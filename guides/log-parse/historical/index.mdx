Orbiter's historical parse runs once per Nylas grant — when a user first connects an email account, or when they trigger a manual re-sync. It reads everything: every sent and received email thread, every calendar event, and every participant. The output feeds `email_account_import`, `email_activity`, `my_person`, and eventually the Known relationship graph.

Two parallel scan surfaces run under the same orchestrator:

## Where the two pipelines overlap

Both pipelines converge on the same downstream surface:

- **`mvp/activity/add-email-account-record` (#12539)** is the single-record gatekeeper for BOTH sent-thread email discovery and calendar-event participant discovery. It's described in full on the [email page](/guides/log-parse/historical/email#add-email-account-record-12539) and is shared verbatim by the calendar flow.
- **`email_account_import`** is the single import table both scan paths write to.
- **`mvp/import/add-people-from-email-account` (#10517)** is the downstream batch processor that turns `email_account_import` rows into `master_person` records. It does not care whether a row came from an email or a calendar event — both flow through the same `get-add/master-person` + signature-extraction path.

In other words: **discovery is split by source, but import and master-person creation are shared**. The division of these pages reflects discovery, not the full lifecycle.
