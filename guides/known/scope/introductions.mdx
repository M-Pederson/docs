---
title: "Introduction Nodes"
description: "Functions for creating, inferring, and managing Introduction nodes — the N-ary record of who introduced whom"
---

Introductions are represented as first-class `Introduction` nodes (not edges) because FalkorDB cannot represent an edge pointing at another edge, and because an introduction is naturally N-ary (one introducer, multiple participants, one resulting `KNOWS`).

See [Schema Design — Introduction](/guides/known/schema-design#introduction) for the full node shape and edge patterns.

---

## `known/introductions/create_introduction`

Creates an `Introduction` node and its edges:
- `(Introducer:Person)-[:MADE_INTRODUCTION]->(Introduction)`
- `(Introduction)-[:CONNECTS]->(Person)` for each participant

Sets `resulting_knows_uuid` if a `KNOWS` edge already exists or is created as a result of the introduction.

**Inputs:** `introducer_uuid`, `participant_uuids[]`, `introduced_at`, `context`, `introduction_medium`, `source`, `confidence`
**Outputs:** `introduction_uuid`
**Side effects:**
- If this is the first evidence of a relationship between the participants, also creates the `KNOWS` edge between them (via the normal ingest pipeline).
- Updates `KNOWS.introduced_by` to the introducer uuid as a denormalized fast-access field pointing to the primary introducer.

## `known/introductions/infer_from_email_cc`

Detects warm introductions in email patterns. Looks for:
- CC patterns where the sender cc's two people who don't have prior direct interaction
- Language cues: "meet X", "X meet Y", "I'd like to connect you two", "wanted to introduce you", etc.
- Subject-line hints ("intro:", "introduction")
- Reply patterns confirming the introduction ("thanks for the intro")

Calls `create_introduction` with `source='inferred'` when the classifier is confident enough. Runs **as part of the email ingest pipeline**, not as a standalone cron — it's triggered per email by the Nylas `message-created` handler.

**Inputs:** email payload (sender, cc list, body, timestamp)
**Outputs:** `introduction_uuid` or null

## `known/introductions/delete_introduction`

Deletes an `Introduction` node and its edges. Used **only** for data-quality cleanup — introductions are historical facts and should not be invalidated on relationship state changes. A falling-out doesn't undo the fact that Patricia introduced Alice to Bob in 2019.

**Inputs:** `introduction_uuid`, `deletion_reason`, `deleted_by`
**Outputs:** count of deleted edges

---

## Design notes

- **Introductions are not invalidated.** They have no `valid_to` concept. If an introduction is wrong (data quality), it's deleted. Otherwise it stays forever.
- **Multiple introductions per pair are allowed.** If Patricia introduced Alice and Bob in 2019, and then Marcus re-introduced them more formally in 2023, both `Introduction` nodes exist and point at the same `KNOWS` edge.
- **`KNOWS.introduced_by` stores the primary introducer** as a denormalized fast-access field. When there are multiple introductions, the earliest is the primary. Full history lives on the Introduction nodes.
- **Group introductions work naturally.** An introduction can have more than 2 `CONNECTS` edges — e.g. "Patricia introduced Alice, Bob, and Carol to each other at a dinner party." Creates a single Introduction node with 3 CONNECTS edges.
