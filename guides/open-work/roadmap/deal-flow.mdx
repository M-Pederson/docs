A best-in-class deal flow interface for visualizing, managing, and tracking investment opportunities and pipelines. This module adds Deal/Opportunity entity tracking with kanban, list, and network visualization surfaces.

## Overview

Deal flow is the lifeblood of venture and growth operations. Orbiter's existing relationship graph (people, companies, signals) provides rich context for deal evaluation and tracking. The deal flow module extends that with:

- **Opportunity entity** — Deal/Opportunity nodes in FalkorDB, linked to Company, Person, and Signal nodes
- **Kanban board** — Drag-and-drop pipeline stages (Prospect → Warm → Active → Closed Won/Lost)
- **List/forecast view** — Tabular pipeline data with sorting, filtering, and metrics
- **Network visualization** — Deal as relationship hub (deal ↔ stakeholders ↔ org chart ↔ prior relationships)

## How Deal Flow Works

Deal flow is the process of tracking opportunities through a pipeline—from initial prospect to closed deal. It answers fundamental business questions:

- **Sales:** "Where are we in the customer acquisition cycle? What's our forecast for this quarter?"
- **Fundraising:** "What's our active runway of investor conversations? Who's warm vs. cold?"
- **Partnerships:** "How many strategic partnerships are we actively closing?"

### The Pipeline: Typical Deal Stages

Deals move through **discrete stages** as they progress. Each stage represents a commitment level or milestone:

```
Prospect → Warm Lead → Active Negotiation → Closed Won / Closed Lost
```

| Stage | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Prospect** | Initial target identified, outreach planned | "Add TechCorp to prospecting list; found contact via LinkedIn" |
| **Warm Lead** | First touch made, early interest signals | "Sarah Chen (TechCorp CEO) replied to my email; agreed to 30-min call" |
| **Active Negotiation** | Deep engagement, terms in discussion | "Sent term sheet; waiting on legal review; expected decision by end of month" |
| **Closed Won** | Deal completed, signed | "Contract signed; SOW kicked off; first payment received" |
| **Closed Lost** | Opportunity concluded without deal | "Prospect chose competitor; budget cycle deferred" |

### Why Deal Flow Tracking Matters

**Visibility & Forecasting**
- You can't manage what you don't measure. Deal flow boards show the entire pipeline at a glance
- Teams can forecast revenue/outcomes by counting deals in each stage and applying win probability
- Example: "We have 8 deals in active negotiation with average deal size $500K. If 50% close, that's $2M in this cycle"

**Bottleneck Detection**
- If deals are stalling in "warm lead" for 60+ days, something's broken (follow-up cadence, qualification process, etc.)
- Example: "Why are 12 deals stuck in 'active negotiation' for 90 days? Do we need to escalate or disqualify?"

**Team Accountability**
- Each deal has an owner (salesperson, investor relations, partnership manager). Deal flow boards clarify who owns what
- Example: "Deal #47 (TechCorp) is owned by Sarah; she should move it from 'warm' to 'active' by Friday"

**Relationship Context**
- Who at the prospect company do we have relationships with? Who can make a warm intro? This is where Orbiter shines
- Example: "TechCorp's CFO went to Stanford with our CEO. We can use that as the warm intro path"

## Use Cases: Sales vs. Fundraising Pipelines

### Sales Pipeline Example

**Scenario:** B2B SaaS company selling project management software to mid-market enterprises

**Deal flow board shows:**

```
Prospect (6)     Warm Lead (8)    Negotiation (4)    Closed Won (2)    Closed Lost (1)
├─ Acme Corp     ├─ TechCorp      ├─ BuildCo         ├─ DataFlow Inc   └─ CloudStart
├─ FastLogic     ├─ StreamFlow    ├─ NextGen         │   (signed)       (chose competitor)
├─ VentureCo     ├─ LogisticsPro  ├─ RetailPlus      └─ CapitalOne
├─ MediaNet      ├─ SaaS Metrics  └─ FactoryOS       (signed)
├─ FinanceFlow   ├─ DigitalShift
└─ OptiMind      └─ (6 more)
```

**Day-to-day usage:**

1. **Monday morning standup:** "We have $3.2M in closed won this month, $2.8M in active negotiation. Expected close: $1.8M by month-end if TechCorp and BuildCo both sign."

2. **Deal owner updates:** Sarah (TechCorp owner) moves TechCorp from "Warm Lead" to "Active Negotiation" after contract review begins. Board auto-updates, team sees progress.

3. **Bottleneck detection:** Ops notices 3 deals have been "warm" for 90+ days. Triggers sales quality review. Did we disqualify these? Do we need to re-engage?

4. **Relationship context:** When prospecting Acme Corp, Sarah checks Orbiter: "Our VP of Eng went to Carnegie Mellon with Acme's VP of Product. Let's use that for a warm intro instead of cold email."

5. **Forecast accuracy:** By week 3 of the month, sales leader pulls the list view, filters to "expected close this month," and reports realistic forecast to finance.

### Fundraising Pipeline Example

**Scenario:** Venture-backed startup raising a Series B ($10M round)

**Deal flow board shows:**

```
Prospect (18)      Warm (12)          Diligence (5)      Term Sheet (2)     Closed (1)
├─ Sequoia         ├─ a16z            ├─ Benchmark       ├─ Lightspeed      ├─ Founders Fund
├─ Andreessen      ├─ Sapphire         ├─ Khosla          ├─ General Catalyst │  ($8M commitment,
├─ Greylock        ├─ Bain Capital     ├─ Accel           │                    │   signed)
├─ Insight         ├─ Menlo Ventures   ├─ Bessemer        └─ Battery
├─ Canvas          ├─ Norwest          └─ SignalFire         (term sheet
├─ GGV             ├─ Bessemer          signed; in
├─ (12 more VCs)   └─ (5 more)          diligence)
```

**Day-to-day usage:**

1. **Momentum tracking:** Founder CEO can see at a glance: 2 term sheets out, 5 in active diligence, 12 warm leads. "We're in a good position; we have redundancy in the pipeline."

2. **Warm intro prioritization:** CEO checks Orbiter: "Benchmark's partner went to Harvard with our board chair. Let's leverage that for a warm intro instead of cold email." Moves Benchmark from "Prospect" to "Warm" immediately after intro made.

3. **Diligence orchestration:** Benchmark is in diligence (legal review, reference calls, customer interviews). Founder drags it from "Warm" to "Diligence," which triggers a diligence timeline checklist. CFO knows to schedule financial deep-dives.

4. **Decision timeline:** Lightspeed and General Catalyst both have term sheets out. Both expected to decide by end of month. Founder uses list view to filter "term sheet stage," sorts by "decision deadline," and flags priority follow-ups.

5. **Relationship stalling:** Sapphire (warm lead) has been in that stage for 45 days. Founder checks: "Last touch was 2 weeks ago. Need to re-engage. Let me check if anyone on the team has another contact at Sapphire." Orbiter shows a relationship to Sapphire's talent partner via a mutual connection. CEO makes a warm intro to move things forward.

6. **Board reporting:** Monthly board meeting. Founder pulls the deal flow board and list view:
   - "Closed: $8M from Founders Fund ✅"
   - "In term sheet stage: $5M+ potential from 2 VCs"
   - "In diligence: $12M+ from 5 VCs"
   - "Total pipeline: $40M+ (including prospects)"
   - "Weighted forecast (50% diligence, 30% term sheet, 10% prospect): $8.5M additional by month-end"

## Key Metrics from Deal Flow

Once you're tracking deals in stages, these metrics become natural to calculate:

| Metric | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Pipeline value** | Total dollar value of all open deals | "$50M pipeline across 40 deals" |
| **Weighted forecast** | Pipeline value × win probability per stage | "$8.5M forecast = ($5M × 70% diligence) + ($3M × 50% term sheet)" |
| **Stage age** | How long deals sit in each stage | "TechCorp has been in negotiation for 32 days; typical close time is 25 days" |
| **Win rate** | % of deals that close won vs. total closed | "We closed 8 of 10 deals last quarter = 80% win rate" |
| **Sales cycle length** | Average days from prospect to closed won | "Average sales cycle is 87 days" |
| **Deal velocity** | How quickly deals move between stages | "Warm leads take avg 14 days to reach negotiation" |

## How Orbiter Amplifies Deal Flow

Standard deal tracking (Salesforce, Pipedrive) manages the pipeline mechanics. Orbiter adds **relationship intelligence**:

- **Warm intro paths:** "Who at Prospect Corp knows someone on our team?" Orbiter's relationship graph answers this automatically
- **Signal integration:** Market intel, news mentions, and people movements for target companies surface deal risk/opportunity
- **Historical context:** "Have we worked with this company before? Via which relationship?"
- **Org chart visibility:** "Who's the actual decision-maker at this company? Let's map it before we pitch"

Example: When you drag a deal from "Prospect" to "Warm," Orbiter could auto-suggest:
- "3 people on our team have a connection to this company's leadership"
- "Recent news: This company just raised $50M, may be hiring aggressively (signal of growth)"
- "Similar deal closed in 45 days 2 years ago with the same stakeholder profile"

This transforms deal flow from a CRM activity log into a **relationship-powered pipeline platform**.

## Architecture: Embed Deal Flow UI Directly

The recommended path: extend Orbiter's existing FalkorDB entities with a Deal/Opportunity node type and layer best-in-class UI surfaces on top.

**Why direct embedding:**
- You already have people, companies, and signals in the graph
- No third-party deal tracking system integration needed
- Single source of truth — relationships and deal context live together
- Extensible — new signals and relationships feed deal insights automatically

## UI Surface Options & Libraries

### 1. Kanban Board (Pipeline View)

A drag-and-drop interface showing deals across pipeline stages. The standard for deal-tracking UX.

#### Implementation Choices:

**Option A: dnd-kit** (Modern, Flexible)
- **Library:** `@dnd-kit/sortable` for kanban column/card mechanics
- **Best for:** Custom styling, complete control over behavior
- **Pairing:** Use your own `Card` and `Column` components
- **Learning curve:** Medium — requires building the interaction layer yourself
- **Example use case:** Orbiter's kanban with shadcn cards, custom stage labels, deal metadata preview

```tsx
// Pseudocode structure
<DndContext>
  {stages.map(stage => (
    <KanbanColumn stage={stage} deals={deals[stage.id]}>
      {deals[stage.id].map(deal => (
        <SortableCard deal={deal} />
      ))}
    </KanbanColumn>
  ))}
</DndContext>
```

**Option B: @hello-pangea/dnd** (Trello-like, Out-of-the-box)
- **Library:** Maintained fork of `react-beautiful-dnd`
- **Best for:** Trello-style kanban feel with minimal setup
- **Pairing:** Works well with shadcn or custom components
- **Learning curve:** Low — opinionated defaults, familiar API
- **Example use case:** Orbiter deal pipeline that "just works" like Trello

**Option C: react-kanban-kit** (Lightweight, Modern)
- **Library:** Built on Atlassian's `pragmatic-drag-and-drop`
- **Best for:** Middle ground — good defaults + customization
- **Pairing:** Pairs well with TanStack Table and React Flow
- **Learning curve:** Low-to-medium
- **Example use case:** Orbiter kanban with future extensibility to network visualization

#### Visual Reference:

A deal flow kanban board typically shows:
- **Column headers** (Prospect, Warm Lead, Active Negotiation, Closed Won, Closed Lost)
- **Cards per deal** with:
  - Deal name / target company
  - Deal size / valuation
  - Lead contact photo
  - Stage age (days in stage)
  - Stakeholder avatars (Orbiter relationship context)
  - Action buttons (view, move, edit)
- **Drag zones** — drop deals to move between stages
- **Collapse/expand** — per-stage view control

### 2. List / Forecast View

A tabular view complementing the kanban, optimized for metrics and decision-making.

#### Implementation:

**TanStack Table** (formerly React Table)
- **Pairs with:** shadcn data-table component
- **Features:** Sorting, filtering, pagination, column visibility, export-to-CSV
- **Example columns:**
  - Deal name / Target company
  - Stage
  - Deal size / Valuation
  - Win probability (from signals)
  - Expected close date
  - Lead contact
  - Last activity

```tsx
// Using TanStack Table + shadcn
<DataTable 
  columns={dealColumns} 
  data={deals}
  filterableColumns={['stage', 'probability']}
  sortableColumns={['dealSize', 'expectedClose']}
/>
```

#### Visual Reference:

A deal flow forecast table typically shows:
- **Left pane:** Deal list with name, company, stage (color-coded)
- **Right pane:** Detailed row view with deal metadata, stakeholders, activity timeline
- **Summary footer:** Total pipeline value, weighted forecast, stage breakdowns
- **Filters:** By stage, probability range, lead owner, date range
- **Exports:** CSV, PDF pipeline report

### 3. Network Visualization (Optional, Future)

For viewing deals as relationship hubs — connecting deal to stakeholders, org chart, prior relationships.

#### Implementation:

**React Flow (xyflow)**
- **Canonical choice** for relationship networks in React
- **Why here:** Your graph backend naturally maps to node-link visualization
- **Future use case:** Show deal context — "Who at the target company has prior relationships with our team? Who's a warm intro path?"

```tsx
// Visualize: Deal ↔ Stakeholders ↔ Org Chart ↔ Prior Relationships
<ReactFlow
  nodes={[
    { id: 'deal-123', label: 'Deal: $5M Series A', type: 'deal' },
    { id: 'person-456', label: 'Sarah Chen (CEO)', type: 'stakeholder' },
    { id: 'company-789', label: 'Target Corp', type: 'company' },
    // ...prior relationship nodes
  ]}
  edges={[
    { source: 'deal-123', target: 'company-789', label: 'targets' },
    { source: 'person-456', target: 'company-789', label: 'works_at' },
    // ...relationship edges from FalkorDB
  ]}
/>
```

#### Visual Reference:

A deal network view typically shows:
- **Center node:** The deal (highlighted, larger)
- **First ring:** Target company, deal stakeholders (CEO, CFO, etc.)
- **Second ring:** Your team members with prior relationships to target
- **Edges:** Labeled with relationship type and strength
- **Interactive:** Click nodes to drill into relationship details, prior context

## Recommended Implementation Path

### Phase 1: Kanban + List (MVP)

1. **Extend graph schema** — Add `Deal` / `Opportunity` node type to FalkorDB
   - Properties: `name`, `target_company_id`, `stage`, `deal_size`, `created_at`, `expected_close`
2. **Create Deal API endpoints** — GET /deals, POST /deals, PATCH /deals/:id, DELETE /deals/:id
3. **Build kanban UI** — Choose **@hello-pangea/dnd** for fastest MVP (Trello-like feel, minimal setup)
4. **Build list view** — TanStack Table + shadcn data-table with stage/probability filters

**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks for a functional deal pipeline

### Phase 2: Network Visualization (Optional)

5. **Add React Flow** — Render deal context as relationship network
6. **Query design** — Cypher queries to fetch deal ↔ stakeholder ↔ org chart ↔ prior relationships
7. **Integration** — Toggle between kanban/list/network views

**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks for functional prototype

## FalkorDB Schema Extensions

### Deal Node

```cypher
CREATE CONSTRAINT deal_unique_id ON (d:Deal) ASSERT d.id IS UNIQUE;
CREATE INDEX FOR (d:Deal) ON (d.stage);
CREATE INDEX FOR (d:Deal) ON (d.created_at);
```

### Deal Relationships

```cypher
// Deal targets a Company
(Deal)-[TARGETS]->(Company)

// Person is stakeholder in Deal
(Deal)-[STAKEHOLDER {role: 'CEO|CFO|etc'}]->(Person)

// Deal has associated Signals (market intel, warm intros, etc.)
(Deal)-[INFORMED_BY]->(Signal)

// Prior relationship context
(Person:YourTeam)-[HAS_PRIOR_RELATIONSHIP]->(Person:TargetCompany)
```

## Comparison: Embed vs. Third-party Systems

| Aspect | Embed in Orbiter | Third-party (Salesforce, Notion, Airtable) |
|--------|------------------|-------|
| **Data model** | Native FalkorDB with relationship context | External CRM, limited relationship depth |
| **Single source of truth** | Yes — graph is canonical | No — separate system, sync overhead |
| **Signal integration** | Automatic — signals feed deal insights | Manual sync required |
| **Customization** | Full control — build what you need | Limited — bound by platform constraints |
| **Relationship context** | Rich — prior connections, org charts, signals | Basic — contact lists only |
| **Network visualization** | Natural fit for React Flow + graph backend | Not standard, requires custom work |
| **Setup time** | 2–4 weeks MVP + list/kanban | 1–2 weeks setup, months for customization |
