**Graph Database Edge Weights** — the master reference for every weighted relationship in the Orbiter graph, and the canonical scale for assigning weights to new edges.

<Note>
  **Lower weight = stronger / more important connection.** Weights are relative ranking values for graph traversal, not literal distances. Use the [Weight scale](#weight-scale) below when adding a new edge.
</Note>

## Weight scale

Pick the band that matches how strong a signal the relationship is for connecting two entities, then fine-tune within the band.

| Band | Weight | Signal strength | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Defining** | 1–5 | Identity-defining bonds | `COFOUNDED_WITH`, `FOUNDED`, `IDENTICAL_TWINS`, `CONNECTED_ON_ORBITER` |
| **Very strong** | 6–15 | Active senior roles, close ties, direct money | `BOARD_MEMBER_OF`, `MENTOR`, `INVESTED_IN` (direct), `RAISED` |
| **Strong** | 16–25 | Standing roles & partnerships | `MEMBER_OF`, `INVESTMENT_PARTNER_AT`, co-investing |
| **Moderate** | 26–40 | Past roles, deal participation, education, expertise | `WORKS_AT` (past), `HAS_EXPERTISE`, `ATTENDED` |
| **Weak** | 41–65 | Acquaintance, co-attendance, co-location, platform | `KNOWS`, `REGISTERED_FOR`, geography |
| **Negligible** | 100 | Platform-only, zero interaction (normalized) | `MUTUALLY_FOLLOWS`, `CONNECTED_ON_LINKEDIN` |

**Principles**

- Direct creation (founding / co-founding) and immediate family are the strongest — nothing should out-rank them.
- Current relationships beat past ones; senior roles beat junior; active interaction beats passive association.
- **Hub nodes** (cities, awards, expertise, events, publications) connect huge numbers of entities. Their edges must sit in **Weak or below**, regardless of how "specific" they feel — a low weight on a hub edge makes every co-member spuriously close.
- Never use `0` or sub-`1` weights — they create near-free traversal and distort every path that touches them. The floor for a real edge is `1`.

**Rationale for the resolved values (the recommendations gave ranges):**

- **Awards / credits → Moderate band (30–40).** Sized so co-credit lands alongside `WORKS_AT` (past) / `HAS_EXPERTISE` / `ATTENDED` — present but never out-ranking founding or family. `WON_AWARD` (30) is slightly stronger than `NOMINATED_FOR` (35) — winning is a tighter tie than a nomination — and `HAS_AWARD` (40, the Film→Award hub edge) is the weakest of the set. `WORKED_ON` = 30 also fixes the 0.5 that was an outright error vs its spec intent.
- **Investment-round monotonicity restored.** Conviction now orders correctly: `LEAD_INVESTED_IN` (15) < `INVESTED_IN` (20) ≤ `FOLLOW_ON_INVESTED_IN` (20) — leading a round is the strongest signal (lowest weight) and a follow-on is no weaker than the initial check. Angel `INVESTED_IN` → Funding_Round stays **25**; Person → Company `INVESTED_IN` stays **15**.
- **`LOCATED_IN` demoted to Weak.** City is a massive hub; at 10 everyone in a city was near-maximally linked. Now **70** (city / region / state) and **80** (direct Person → Country, an even bigger hub = weaker tie). The `IN_REGION` (25) / `IN_COUNTRY` (50) taxonomy backbone is unchanged — those are structural geography edges, not affinity signals.
- **`HAS_SUBDOMAIN` = 100.** Taxonomy edges should never shorten a path between two people; an explicit Negligible weight removes the `null`-in-path-math ambiguity without marking the edge non-traversable.

### KNOWS weight lifecycle

`KNOWS` is **evidence-driven**: it is created at a weak default and only strengthens (weight *lowers*) as interaction data accumulates. Weights ratchet **downward** from the default — they never start strong.

1. **Created at the default.** When Orbiter first detects a relationship with no interaction history, the edge is written at the **default weight `60`** (the weakest end of the band). This is why every live `KNOWS` is currently 60 — none have been decayed yet. **Confirmed in the creator** `known/ingest/create_knows_edge` (#12778), which writes `k.weight = 60` on `ON CREATE` and leaves the scoring fields null for the re-scoring cron to fill — so the §3.6 audit required **no fix-forward change** to the KNOWS creator.
2. **Crons lower it over time.** Background jobs re-score the edge as evidence lands — 1:1 emails, calendar events, reply rates, recency — and reduce the weight toward the floor. **Status:** the re-scoring cron that ratchets the weight down is a tracked **follow-up** — not yet built. Until it ships, every `KNOWS` stays at the 60 default (correct creation state, just not yet decayed).
3. **Floor ~8.** Even a maximally-active `KNOWS` stops around `BOARD_MEMBER_OF` / `MENTOR` strength, staying **below** the structural bonds (`FOUNDED` 5, family 1–10).

| Accumulated signal | Weight |
| --- | --- |
| No interaction data yet *(creation default)* | 60 |
| Minimal contact | 50 |
| Light contact (occasional email, or 1 event) | 40 |
| Regular contact (frequent email, or repeat events) | 25 |
| Close (high 1:1 email **and** multiple events) | 8–10 |

<Note>
  Filter automated / group / newsletter mail before it counts toward strengthening — raw volume over-inflates the signal. This **default-then-decay** pattern applies to any evidence-scored edge, not just `KNOWS`: create at the weakest default, let crons earn the weight down.
</Note>

### WORKS_AT weight buckets

`WORKS_AT` is the one professional edge whose weight is **computed, not a constant** — it folds *seniority* and *current-vs-past tenure* into a single value, which is why the live graph shows 14 distinct weights across the 5–40 range. The §3.6 audit asked for this mapping to be published rather than changed; the values are already canonical (no fix-forward, no backfill).

The formula in `mvp/edges/create-work-edges` (#2800) is:

```
weight = seniority_rank + (any role at this company is current ? 0 : 20)
```

`seniority_rank` is derived from the best (most senior, current-preferred) role title:

| Seniority (best role title) | rank | Current weight | Past weight (+20) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| C-Suite — CEO/CTO/CFO/COO/CMO/CRO/CPO/CIO/CISO, or "Chief … Officer" | 5 | **5** | **25** |
| Founder / Co-Founder | 8 | **8** | **28** |
| Operating Partner (general / managing partner — *not* Limited Partner) | 8 | **8** | **28** |
| President (not Vice President) | 8 | **8** | **28** |
| SVP / EVP | 10 | **10** | **30** |
| VP / Vice President | 12 | **12** | **32** |
| Director / Head of | 15 | **15** | **35** |
| Manager / Lead / Principal | 18 | **18** | **38** |
| Individual Contributor (default) | 20 | **20** | **40** |

So a current C-suite role is the strongest `WORKS_AT` (5) and a past individual-contributor role is the weakest (40, the largest live bucket). The classifier also routes Founder titles to a parallel `FOUNDED` edge (weight 5), and Limited Partner / Angel / Advisor / Board / union-member titles to their specialized edges instead of `WORKS_AT` — see [edge-audit §3.3 / §3.7](/guides/robert-mark/edge-audit).

<Note>
  **Known quirk (out of scope for §3.6):** a *current* C-suite `WORKS_AT` lands at weight 5, tying `FOUNDED` in the **Defining** band. A future refinement could floor non-founder employment at the Very-strong band so founding stays strictly strongest; deferred so this pass changes only the values the audit flagged.
</Note>
