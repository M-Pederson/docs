**Graph Database Edge Weights** — the master reference for every weighted relationship in the Orbiter graph, and the canonical scale for assigning weights to new edges.

<Note>
  **Lower weight = stronger / more important connection.** Weights are relative ranking values for graph traversal, not literal distances. Use the [Weight scale](#weight-scale) below when adding a new edge.
</Note>

## Weight scale

Pick the band that matches how strong a signal the relationship is for connecting two entities, then fine-tune within the band.

| Band | Weight | Signal strength | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Defining** | 1–5 | Identity-defining bonds | `COFOUNDED_WITH`, `FOUNDED`, `IDENTICAL_TWINS`, `CONNECTED_ON_ORBITER` |
| **Very strong** | 6–15 | Active senior roles, close ties, direct money | `BOARD_MEMBER_OF`, `MENTOR`, `INVESTED_IN` (direct), `RAISED` |
| **Strong** | 16–25 | Standing roles & partnerships | `MEMBER_OF`, `INVESTMENT_PARTNER_AT`, co-investing |
| **Moderate** | 26–40 | Past roles, deal participation, education, expertise | `WORKS_AT` (past), `HAS_EXPERTISE`, `ATTENDED` |
| **Weak** | 41–65 | Acquaintance, co-attendance, co-location, platform | `KNOWS`, `REGISTERED_FOR`, geography |
| **Negligible** | 100 | Platform-only, zero interaction (normalized) | `MUTUALLY_FOLLOWS`, `CONNECTED_ON_LINKEDIN` |

**Principles**

- Direct creation (founding / co-founding) and immediate family are the strongest — nothing should out-rank them.
- Current relationships beat past ones; senior roles beat junior; active interaction beats passive association.
- **Hub nodes** (cities, awards, expertise, events, publications) connect huge numbers of entities. Their edges must sit in **Weak or below**, regardless of how "specific" they feel — a low weight on a hub edge makes every co-member spuriously close.
- Never use `0` or sub-`1` weights — they create near-free traversal and distort every path that touches them. The floor for a real edge is `1`.

**Notes on weights**

- `[:WORKS_AT]` spans **5–40** across 14 distinct weights — it folds seniority *and* current-vs-past into one edge. There is no separate `WORKED_AT`; **past roles live here at weight ~40** (the 40 bucket, 2,385 edges, is the largest).
- `[:HAS_EXPERTISE]` spans **22–50** (a relevance / confidence gradient) and is the highest-volume edge by far (16,695).
- `[:INVESTED_IN]` weight is pattern-dependent: **15** Person→Company, **20** VC_Firm→Funding_Round, **25** Person/Angel→Funding_Round.
- `[:LOCATED_IN]` is mostly **10** (11,925 edges); 373 carry **50**. `VOLUNTEERED_AT` is **38** (51) or **50** (29). `AUTHORED` is **15 / 18 / 25** (mostly 25).

### Weight audit — recommended adjustments

<Warning>
  **Hub-node edges are the strongest weights in the graph, which inverts relationship ranking.** The lowest weights all sit on high-degree hub nodes — awards (`0.3`–`0.7`) and city via `LOCATED_IN` (`10`). That makes any two people who share an award or a city *more strongly connected than co-founders* (`FOUNDED` = 5). If these edges participate in path / affinity scoring, raise them into the Weak band; if they are attribute-only and excluded from traversal, document that exclusion.
</Warning>

| Edge(s) | Live | Recommended | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `WON_AWARD` / `NOMINATED_FOR` / `HAS_AWARD` / `WORKED_ON` | 0.3–0.7 | 25–40 | Award / credit nodes are hubs; sub-`1` weights make co-credit the strongest tie in the graph, out-ranking founding and family. `WORKED_ON` at 0.5 vs its spec value of 15 looks like an outright error. |
| `LEAD_INVESTED_IN` | 30 | ≤ 15 | Leading a round is the **strongest** investment signal but is currently the **weakest** of the three round edges. Must be lower than `INVESTED_IN` (20) and `FOLLOW_ON_INVESTED_IN` (25). |
| `FOLLOW_ON_INVESTED_IN` | 25 | ≤ 20 | Following on signals added conviction — should not be weaker than the initial check (`INVESTED_IN` 20). |
| `LOCATED_IN` | 10 | 60–80 | City is a massive hub and co-location is a weak signal; at 10, everyone in a city is near-maximally linked. |
| `WAS_FOUNDED` | 0 | remove | Zero-weight edge on the `contextFounded` artifact (below). Not a real relationship. |
| `KNOWS` | 60 (flat) | 60 default → ~8 floor | Flat 60 discards the interaction signal. It should be **born at the 60 default and decay toward ~8** as evidence accrues — see [KNOWS weight lifecycle](#knows-weight-lifecycle). |
| `HAS_SUBDOMAIN` | `null` | explicit value | A `null` weight is ambiguous in path math. Give taxonomy edges an explicit high weight or mark them non-traversable. |
| `WORKS_AT` | 5–40 | document buckets | The most important professional edge encodes seniority + tenure in 14 weights; publish the weight → (seniority, tenure) mapping so it is reproducible. |

<Warning>
  `contextFounded` shows up as a `[:FOUNDED]` **target** (17 edges) and as the `[:WAS_FOUNDED]` source — it looks like a mislabeled / placeholder node, not a real entity. Investigate and clean up before treating `WAS_FOUNDED` as a deployed edge.
</Warning>

### KNOWS weight lifecycle

`KNOWS` is **evidence-driven**: it is created at a weak default and only strengthens (weight *lowers*) as interaction data accumulates. Weights ratchet **downward** from the default — they never start strong.

1. **Created at the default.** When Orbiter first detects a relationship with no interaction history, the edge is written at the **default weight `60`** (the weakest end of the band). This is why every live `KNOWS` is currently 60 — none have been decayed yet.
2. **Crons lower it over time.** Background jobs re-score the edge as evidence lands — 1:1 emails, calendar events, reply rates, recency — and reduce the weight toward the floor.
3. **Floor ~8.** Even a maximally-active `KNOWS` stops around `BOARD_MEMBER_OF` / `MENTOR` strength, staying **below** the structural bonds (`FOUNDED` 5, family 1–10).

| Accumulated signal | Weight |
| --- | --- |
| No interaction data yet *(creation default)* | 60 |
| Minimal contact | 50 |
| Light contact (occasional email, or 1 event) | 40 |
| Regular contact (frequent email, or repeat events) | 25 |
| Close (high 1:1 email **and** multiple events) | 8–10 |

<Note>
  Filter automated / group / newsletter mail before it counts toward strengthening — raw volume over-inflates the signal. This **default-then-decay** pattern applies to any evidence-scored edge, not just `KNOWS`: create at the weakest default, let crons earn the weight down.
</Note>
