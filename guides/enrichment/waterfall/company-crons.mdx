One scheduled task serves the company waterfall: the queue-drain cron **Task #127**. It ships `active = false` on live, so queued depth-1 leaves do not drain automatically — the operator batch drain [`process-enrichment-queue` #12816](/guides/enrichment/waterfall/company-functions#queue--gate-infrastructure) is the manual alternative. Queue semantics: [Company Waterfall overview](/guides/enrichment/waterfall/company-waterfall#queue--upsert-semantics) · queue schema: [Company Waterfall Tables](/guides/enrichment/waterfall/company-tables).

<Card icon="clock-rotate-left">
  ```text
  process-company-queue — Task #127
  ```

  **Single-row tick on `queue_enrich_company`, every 600 s.** Pick: `processing == false` sorted `priority_tier asc, cascade_depth asc, id asc` (lowest tier, then shallowest depth, then FIFO) → lock `processing: true` (advisory — not atomic with the pick) → **sync** #12992 with the row's `cascade_depth` → delete on success / `log_crash` \+ release on failure. There is no retry counter and no dead-letter: with stable sort keys, a deterministically-crashing row is re-picked on the very next tick, forever — head-of-line poison-pill blocking of the whole queue. No kill-switch check of its own (contrast #12816). **As-built (flagged):** the catch writes `phase: "queue"`, which is outside the strict `log_crash` phase enum — the crash logger itself may fail (open question in the rebuild-spec gaps).
</Card>

<Warning>
  **Task #127 is `active = false` on live** (verified 2026-07-02) — nothing drains the company queue automatically today. Queued depth-1 leaves sit in `queue_enrich_company` until an operator runs `mvp/queue/process-enrichment-queue` #12816. Flip the task active in the Xano UI to enable automatic draining (max throughput: 6 companies/hour at 1 row per 10-minute tick).
</Warning>
