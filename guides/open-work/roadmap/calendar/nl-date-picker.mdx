## One date input, everywhere a date is entered

Orbiter has a date-entry problem hiding in plain sight. The moment the [Calendar](/guides/open-work/roadmap/calendar) ships, an operator will type or pick a date in at least four surfaces: **creating a calendar event** (mirrored to `event` 221 / `master_event` 222), setting a **`df_task` due/start date**, scheduling a **`df_reminder`** nudge, and choosing a **Nylas scheduling** window. Today each of those would grow its own ad-hoc date field — a different popover, a different keyboard story, a different timezone assumption, a different answer to "what does *next Tuesday* mean." That is exactly the fragmentation the Deal Flow design warns against: *one entity, many lenses* — and a date is an entity.

This page scopes **one** component — the **NL Date Picker** — that becomes the single way a human expresses a moment in time across the whole app. It accepts plain language the way a person actually thinks about scheduling:

| Phrase | Parses to | Hard part | Path |
| ------ | --------- | --------- | ---- |
| `next tue 3pm` | a single datetime, local tz | relative weekday + time-of-day, forward-dated | chrono |
| `in 2 weeks` | a date offset from now | relative duration, all-day | chrono |
| `fri–sun` / `9–11am` | a range (`start`+`end`) | two components, one phrase | chrono |
| `the Friday before the board call` | context-relative datetime | needs a calendar anchor ("the board call") | **AI only** |
| `every other Monday` | a recurrence → RRULE | recurrence — chrono cannot do this | **AI only** |
| `noon` / `eod` / `tomorrow morning` | smart-defaulted time-of-day | locale + preference-aware defaults | chrono |

The component is **AI-first by default but never AI-only**: common phrases parse instantly on-device (free, offline, zero-latency), and only the genuinely hard ones escalate to the model. Every parse renders a **confirmable preview** before it commits — human-in-the-loop is non-negotiable, the same ethos the Deal Flow and Calendar designs hold.

The picker exists to remove friction from the operator's scheduling loop, not to showcase parsing tricks. It earns complexity only when it prevents one of three common failures: a slow manual date pick that breaks flow, an ambiguous phrase that commits the wrong time, or a timezone/recurrence mistake that the operator notices too late. The v1 center of gravity is therefore typed/voice input → unambiguous echoed result → one-keystroke confirmation. Availability-aware suggestions, preference memory, and Copilot conversation are useful only when they preserve that short loop instead of replacing it with a chat detour.

## What good looks like — deep UX research synthesis

Before designing, here is the synthesized state of the art in date/time pickers. These are *patterns*, drawn from the products that set the bar (Vimcal, Fantastical, Notion Calendar, Cron, Linear, Google/Outlook, Dub's smart-datetime, the shadcn community date controls, and the Nielsen-Norman / GOV.UK / Adobe-Spectrum date-input guidance). Each maps to a concrete requirement for our control.

| # | Research finding (pattern, not a URL) | Requirement for the NL Date Picker |
| - | ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| 1 | **Default to the user's local timezone, but always *show* the tz** — silent tz assumptions are the #1 scheduling bug. | Resolve tz from `user` (125) `local_timezone` / `settings.working_timezone`; render the tz label beside the result; store IANA + UTC. |
| 2 | **A "Now / Today" shortcut is the most-used control** — one tap to the present. | Persistent `Today` / `Now` affordance + keyboard chip. |
| 3 | **Smart, opinionated defaults beat empty fields** — a bare "3" should mean 3pm in a work context; "noon"/"eod" should resolve sensibly. | Time-of-day defaults conditioned on context (a *due* date defaults to end-of-day, a *meeting* to the next round work hour). |
| 4 | **Typed input first, visual calendar as fallback** — power users type, everyone else clicks; never force the mouse. | A text field is the primary input; the shadcn `Calendar` popover is the secondary visual fallback, not the entry point. |
| 5 | **Spell out months, never all-numeric** — `03/04` is ambiguous across locales; `Mar 4` is not. | Echo the parsed result with a spelled-out month + weekday (`Tue, Mar 4 · 3:00 PM PST`). |
| 6 | **Keyboard-first, like Vimcal** — arrow-to-adjust, type-to-jump, `Esc`/`Enter` discipline, no mouse round-trips. | Full keyboard model: type to parse, `↑/↓` to nudge the active component, `Enter` to confirm, `Esc` to revert. |
| 7 | **Natural-language pioneers (Fantastical) proved free-text scheduling** — "Lunch with Sam Friday at noon" creating a real event is the gold standard. | NL is the *primary* mode, not a hidden power feature. |
| 8 | **Notion Calendar / Cron normalized the cmdk-style quick-add** — a command bar where a date phrase is just another token. | Offer a `⌘K`/cmdk quick-entry surface in addition to the inline field. |
| 9 | **Dub's smart-datetime input** — `chrono` parse + a native `datetime-local` fallback + **echo-on-blur** of the resolved value — is the proven minimal pattern. | Adopt the echo-on-blur and graceful native fallback verbatim as the P1 baseline. |
| 10 | **shadcn `natural-language-datetime-input` / `lingua-time`** community controls prove the chrono-on-shadcn shape works with our exact primitives (`Input`, `Popover`, `Calendar`). | Build on the installed shadcn `input` + `popover` + `calendar`, not a new dependency. |
| 11 | **Accessibility: never color-only, real SR labels, announce the resolved value** — a parse result must reach a screen reader, and validity must not rely on red text alone. | `aria-live` announcement of the echoed result; icon + label state, never color alone; the calendar fallback is fully keyboard-navigable. |
| 12 | **Avoid endless scroll-wheels** — infinite month/year scrollers and tiny time spinners are slow and error-prone. | Type-to-jump over scrolling; the calendar fallback paginates, never infinite-scrolls. |
| 13 | **Mobile: 44px+ touch targets, native pickers where they win** — fat-finger-safe, and don't fight the OS picker on touch. | Touch-sized targets; fall back to the native `datetime-local` control on coarse pointers. |

The throughline: **type a phrase, see it echoed back in unambiguous spelled-out form with its timezone, confirm with one keystroke** — and only reach for a grid when language fails.

## AI-first UI design — concrete and opinionated

Orbiter is not adding "a date field with autocomplete." The control is designed AI-first: the model is a first-class participant in *interpreting intent*, and the UI is built to make that interpretation **transparent, correctable, and reversible** — the same human-in-the-loop spine as the Deal Flow Confirm Sheet and the Calendar `schedule_meeting` verb.

### The interaction spine

1. **Quick entry.** The operator types into an inline field (or a `⌘K` cmdk command-bar token). Input is debounced; nothing commits on keystroke.
2. **Inline ghost-text echo.** As soon as a parse resolves, the field shows a **ghost-text echo** of the interpretation inline — type `next thu`, see `→ Thu, Jun 18` rendered as dimmed trailing text. This is the cheapest possible feedback loop and it is always on.
3. **The Preview Chip (the human-in-the-loop boundary).** Before commit, the resolved value renders as a **confirmable chip**: `Tue, Mar 4 · 3:00 PM PST` with `↵ confirm` / `edit` / `✕`. Nothing writes until the chip is accepted. For ranges and recurrence the chip expands (`Every other Mon · from Jun 16 · no end`). This chip *is* the contract — it is the same object whether a human typed it, voice dictated it, or the Copilot proposed it.
4. **The AI label + explainability popover.** When the AI layer (not chrono) produced the result, the chip carries a small **AI label** (Carbon-Design-style "AI" affordance). Tapping it opens a **"why this time"** popover: the phrase it received, the reference instant it anchored to, the timezone it assumed, what it inferred ("you said *the Friday before the board call* → found *Board call* on Jun 19 → the Friday before is Jun 13"), and its **confidence**. The instant the operator manually overrides any field, the **AI label disappears and the chip reverts to a plain field** — provenance is honest, never sticky.
5. **Confidence + conversational disambiguation.** Low-confidence parses don't guess silently. A `0.62`-confidence result surfaces a one-line disambiguation inline (`Did you mean this Friday (Jun 13) or next (Jun 20)?`) with tappable options; truly ambiguous phrases can hand off to the Copilot for a one-turn clarification rather than a dropdown.
6. **Voice entry.** The existing STT hook (`use-voice-input.ts` — Web Speech API with a Xano `transcribeSpeech` fallback) feeds the same parse pipeline: dictate "remind me the day after the demo," get the same Preview Chip. Voice and text are one path.
7. **Predictive + availability-aware defaults.** Over time the control learns preferred windows (an operator who always books mornings gets `9:30 AM`, not `12:00 PM`, as the default time when they type a bare date). For meetings it can pull **cross-participant availability** (Nylas Free/Busy / Availability) and bias the suggestion toward an open mutual slot — surfacing "everyone's free Thu 2 PM" instead of a blind default.
8. **Theme-awareness.** The control is fully theme-aware: it reads the OKLch design tokens (`--background`, `--foreground`, `--popover`, `--border`, `--muted`, `--primary` — defined in `src/styles.css`) and flips automatically with the `.dark` class via `@custom-variant dark (&:is(.dark *))` — no JS theme switching. The selected accent from `THEME_COLORS` (`src/constants/colors.ts`) drives the today-highlight, the active-component caret, and the confirm affordance, so the picker matches whatever accent the user chose. Validity/confidence states pair an icon + label with color, never color alone (WCAG in both modes).

### Proposal and correction lifecycle

The picker is the smallest version of the calendar's AI trust contract. A parsed date is not just a string; it is an editable proposal with provenance.

| Lifecycle step | UI requirement | Data requirement |
| -------------- | -------------- | ---------------- |
| Draft parse | Show the echoed date, timezone, confidence, and source before commit | Emit `ParsedDate` only; do not write owner state. |
| AI explanation | Show anchor, timezone assumption, recurrence interpretation, and confidence | Explanation may cite only context the viewer can access. |
| Manual correction | Remove AI provenance from edited fields and re-confirm the final value | Log proposed vs accepted value for tuning, with redacted context. |
| Failed parse | Offer visual picker, alternatives, or a one-turn Copilot clarification | No durable change; no write attempt by the parser. |
| Confirmed value | Hand the final `ParsedDate` to the owning surface | The owning surface decides the write path and handles rollback. |

This lifecycle is intentionally the same for typed text, voice, command-bar entry, and Copilot suggestions. Consistency matters more than a clever parsing path: the operator should always know what was inferred, what changed, and what will happen on confirm.

## Architecture — scope all three, recommend the hybrid

The open question the design must settle: **"AI might be better than chrono — go all-AI, or AI + chrono?"** All three architectures are scoped below; the recommendation is the **AI + chrono hybrid**, and the rest of this section makes that decision explicit.

| | **A — All-AI** | **B — AI + chrono hybrid (recommended)** | **C — chrono-only** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Every phrase parses via | the model (Robert/Copilot) | chrono first; AI only on escalation | chrono only |
| Latency (common case) | network round-trip per parse | **zero** (on-device chrono) | zero |
| Cost | token spend per keystroke-batch | tokens only on the hard ~10% | free |
| Offline | fails | **degrades to chrono** | works |
| Arbitrary complexity / context-relative | yes | yes (escalates) | no |
| Recurrence → RRULE | yes | yes (AI tier) | **no** |
| Multilingual | yes | yes (escalates) | limited |
| Learns preferences | yes | yes (AI tier) | no |
| Implementation surface | one path | two paths + a local gate | one path |

### Option A — All-AI

Every phrase goes to an LLM (the existing Robert API `Bd_dCiOz` / Copilot infra) and returns the structured contract directly. **Pros:** handles arbitrary complexity, context-relative phrases, native recurrence → RRULE, multilingual, learns the operator's preferences, and it is *one* code path. **Cons:** a network round-trip (and token cost) on **every** parse — felt as latency on the common "tomorrow" case — it fails offline, and low-confidence results still need confirmation. Defensible if NL coverage matters more than instant feedback, but it pays the model tax even for the trivial 90%. **Kept as a config flag, not the default** (see the force-all-AI flag below).

### Option B — AI + chrono hybrid (recommended default)

`chrono-node` (already installed) parses the common **~90%** instantly, on-device, free, with zero latency — single dates *and* ranges. The system **escalates to the AI tier only when chrono is insufficient**: low-confidence, unparsed, recurring, or context-relative. chrono handles single dates and ranges **only — it has no concept of recurrence, RRULE, or calendar context**, so those always route to AI. This gives the instant-and-free common path *and* the unlimited-complexity hard path, and degrades to chrono-only when offline or when the AI is unavailable. **B *is* C plus an escalation door** — which is exactly why it dominates both alternatives.

<Warning>
**chrono is dates + ranges, full stop.** It will never produce an RRULE, never resolve "the Friday before the board call," and never learn a preference. Treat any recurrence keyword or context-relative phrase as an automatic AI-tier route — do not try to bolt recurrence onto chrono with regex; producing the RRULE is the AI layer's job, not the gate's. The gate only *detects* recurrence to route it.
</Warning>

### Option C — chrono-only

The no-AI baseline: `chrono.parse()` + the shadcn `Calendar` visual fallback. **Pros:** free, offline, instant, trivial to ship. **Cons:** no recurrence, no context, no learning, weaker on messy input. This is not a competing product so much as the **permanent offline/degraded fallback** that option B already contains — which is why C ships as P1 (it is the chrono floor of the hybrid) and then grows the AI tier in P2, rather than being a fork.

### The escalation router — the explicit decision

The user asked specifically about the router, so the decision is stated plainly:

- **There is NO LLM router.** Do not use a model to decide chrono-vs-AI. Paying for a model call to *route* defeats the entire latency/cost saving — if you are already calling a model to decide, you might as well let that model parse, which collapses straight back into all-AI.
- **The router is a free, deterministic, local gate.** It runs chrono first, then escalates only when a cheap on-device check trips — zero network, zero tokens, sub-millisecond.
- **A model-tier router is legitimate, but only INSIDE the AI layer.** Once you've already decided to call the AI, routing between a cheap and a strong model is a quality/cost dial — and it never adds a wasted call because the cheap call *is* the parse.
- **A config flag can force all-AI.** One boolean turns the hybrid into option A for contexts where NL coverage trumps latency. The hybrid is the default; all-AI is one flag away; chrono-only is the offline floor. One component, three behaviors.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The deterministic local gate (runs after chrono, before any AI call)">
    Escalate to the AI tier if **any** of these are true — all are local, zero-cost checks on chrono's own output, no network, no tokens:

    - **chrono returned nothing** — `chrono.parse(text)` is empty.
    - **a key component is uncertain** — chrono exposes `ParsedComponents`; gate on `result.start.isCertain('day') / isCertain('month') / isCertain('hour')`. If a needed component is *implied* rather than *certain*, escalate.
    - **confidence below threshold** — derive a confidence from component certainty + match coverage; below the tuned floor, escalate (and surface the inline disambiguation).
    - **the matched text doesn't cover the input** — `result.text` is much shorter than the trimmed input (chrono grabbed a fragment and ignored the rest → there is meaning it missed).
    - **a tiny regex hits recurrence** — `/\b(every|each|weekly|biweekly|bi-weekly|other|recurring)\b/i`. Recurrence is AI-only.
    - **a tiny regex hits context-relativity** — `/\b(before|after)\b.*\b(the|my|our)\b/i`, or a capitalized proper-noun anchor ("the board call", "earnings") chrono cannot resolve.

    Everything that passes all checks is served by chrono instantly. Nothing reaches the model unless it earns it. The gate is biased *toward* chrono on purpose: a false escalation is wasted latency and money, so it only fires on a real recurrence/context hit or genuine component uncertainty.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Model-tier routing — the only legitimate 'router', and it lives inside the AI layer">
    A router earns its place **after** you've decided to call the AI — as a **model-tier** router for cost control: default to a cheap, fast model, and escalate to a stronger model only when the cheap model returns low confidence. That is a quality/cost dial *inside* the AI layer, not a chrono-vs-AI gate, and it never adds a wasted model call (the cheap call is the parse, and its output is kept if confident). The Robert API group (`Bd_dCiOz`, id 1261) is where this tiering lives — the same infra Meeting Prep already uses.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Config: the force-all-AI flag">
    Keep a config flag to **force all-AI** for environments or contexts where NL coverage trumps latency (multilingual users, or eval/QA of the AI tier). With the flag on, the deterministic gate is bypassed and every parse goes straight to the AI layer (option A). With it off (the default), the hybrid runs. chrono-only is what remains when the network or the Robert API is down. Same component, three behaviors, one flag.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## OpenRouter AI call contract

The AI tier has three possible calls. Only the first two are needed for P2; the third is P3 when Availability enters the picker. All three calls go through `https://openrouter.ai/api/v1/chat/completions`, use strict structured output, and carry the same privacy posture as the rest of workspace 3: `provider.data_collection = "deny"`. Do **not** attach `openrouter:web_search`; the model is interpreting private calendar and task context, not researching the public web.

| Call | When it runs | Default model | Escalation model | Output |
| ---- | ------------ | ------------- | ---------------- | ------ |
| `nl_date_parse` | Deterministic gate escalates recurrence, context-relative, unparsed, or low-confidence input | `openai/gpt-5-nano` | `openai/gpt-5-mini` | One `ParsedDate` plus optional disambiguation choices. |
| `nl_date_disambiguate` | Parser cannot safely pick one interpretation | `openai/gpt-5-nano` | `openai/gpt-5-mini` | Two to four preview-chip choices and one clarification question. |
| `nl_date_slot_suggest` | P3 meeting flow has attendees and redacted Availability windows | `openai/gpt-5-mini` | `anthropic/claude-opus-4.8` for high-value / repeated failure cases | Ranked candidate slots with privacy-safe rationale. |

Alternate cheap/eval model: `deepseek/deepseek-v4-flash` is viable for `nl_date_parse` benchmarks because it is inexpensive and supports structured outputs, but keep `reasoning.enabled = false` and cap output tightly. Orbiter has already seen reasoning models return empty `content` when the completion budget is consumed by reasoning tokens; date parsing does not need hidden chain-of-thought. Re-run a small golden set before making it the default.

Model IDs above were checked against the live OpenRouter model list on **2026-06-16**. Treat them as recommendations, not a pricing contract; before implementation, run the model audit / pricing workflow and keep the cheapest model that clears the golden-set accuracy floor.

### Shared OpenRouter request parameters

```json
{
  "stream": false,
  "temperature": 0,
  "max_completion_tokens": 900,
  "reasoning": { "enabled": false },
  "provider": {
    "data_collection": "deny",
    "require_parameters": true,
    "allow_fallbacks": true,
    "sort": "latency"
  },
  "metadata": {
    "feature": "nl_date_picker",
    "surface": "df_task|df_reminder|calendar_event|nylas_scheduler|cmdk|copilot",
    "call": "nl_date_parse|nl_date_disambiguate|nl_date_slot_suggest"
  }
}
```

Parameter presets:

| Preset | Model | Temperature | `max_completion_tokens` | Provider sort | Notes |
| ------ | ----- | ----------- | ----------------------- | ------------- | ----- |
| Fast parse | `openai/gpt-5-nano` | `0` | `700` | `latency` | Default P2 parse and disambiguation. |
| Strong parse | `openai/gpt-5-mini` | `0` | `900` | `latency` | Retry when fast parse returns low confidence, invalid JSON, or schema-valid but unsafe ambiguity. |
| Slot suggest | `openai/gpt-5-mini` | `0.1` | `1200` | `latency` | P3 Availability-aware suggestions with a ranked list and short rationale. |
| Repair / eval | `deepseek/deepseek-v4-flash` | `0` | `700` | `price` | Cheap benchmark lane; always set `reasoning.enabled = false`. |
| Human-feeling fallback | `anthropic/claude-opus-4.8` | `0.1` | `1200` | `latency` | Use sparingly for high-value Copilot scheduling failures, not routine parsing. |

Implementation rules:

- **Never call the AI per keystroke.** chrono may parse on debounce; AI runs only on blur, `Enter`, explicit "AI parse", Copilot handoff, or a scheduling confirmation step.
- **Always require structured output.** Send `response_format.type = "json_schema"` with `strict: true`; set `provider.require_parameters = true` so OpenRouter avoids providers that do not honor the schema.
- **Treat model output as a proposal.** Validate schema, validate timezone, validate UTC conversion with `@date-fns/tz`, then render the Preview Chip. The owning surface still decides the write path.
- **Fail closed on hidden context.** If the model needs a calendar anchor or teammate detail that is unavailable after `resolve_access()` and the privacy ladder, it returns `needs_confirmation: true` with a clarification, not a guessed date.
- **Log corrections, not raw private context.** Persist proposed value, accepted value, confidence, model id, and redacted reason for tuning; do not store raw teammate event payloads in correction logs.

### Structured response schema

Use the same schema for fast and strong parse calls. The app may strip `alternatives` after a confident parse, but the model should be allowed to return them when ambiguity is real.

```json

  "type": "json_schema",
  "json_schema": {
    "name": "nl_date_parse_result",
    "strict": true,
    "schema": {
      "type": "object",
      "additionalProperties": false,
      "required": [
        "start",
        "end",
        "all_day",
        "rrule",
        "timezone",
        "confidence",
        "needs_confirmation",
        "explanation",
        "source",
        "alternatives",
```

### The parse contract

Whatever the path — chrono, cheap model, strong model, voice, or Copilot — every parse resolves to **one structured object**. This is the seam the whole app codes against; the producer is invisible to the consumer.

```ts
type ParsedDate = {
  start: string;            // ISO-8601, UTC
  end: string | null;       // ISO-8601, UTC — set for ranges
  all_day: boolean;         // true = date-only (chrono component certainty drives this)
  rrule: string | null;     // RFC-5545 RRULE — AI tier ONLY, null from chrono
  timezone: string;         // IANA id (e.g. "America/Los_Angeles"); stored, never assumed
  confidence: number;       // 0–1; low → needs_confirmation + inline disambiguation
  needs_confirmation: boolean; // gates the Preview Chip's commit
  explanation: string;      // "the Friday before Board call (Jun 19) → Jun 13" — powers the AI "why" popover
  source: "chrono" | "ai-fast" | "ai-strong" | "voice"; // provenance for the AI label
};
```

Contract rules, locked:

- **Recurrence is AI-only.** `rrule` is always `null` from chrono. An RRULE writes to `master_event` (222) for calendar **events** (the recurrence master behind the `event` 221 mirror) and to `df_task.recurrence_rule` (NEW, no id) for recurring **tasks** — never a parallel model, matching the Calendar + Deal Flow recurrence contract.
- **Timezones via `date-fns` v4 + `@date-fns/tz` (`TZDate`); store IANA + UTC.** Resolve the tz from `user` (125) `local_timezone` / `settings.working_timezone`; render it on the chip; never store wall-clock alone. (The app's own date math is date-fns v4 — luxon appears *only* as a FullCalendar named-tz render adapter on the calendar canvas, not in this control.)
- **Confirm-before-commit, always.** `needs_confirmation` may relax for high-confidence chrono parses (the chip still shows, `Enter` commits), but nothing writes silently. The Preview Chip is the single human-in-the-loop boundary, mirroring the Deal Flow Confirm Sheet.
- **The parser is not a writer.** The picker emits `ParsedDate`; the consuming surface decides whether that becomes a `df_task` date, a `df_reminder`, or a calendar write. For calendar events, commit still flows through `schedule_meeting` → Nylas → webhook → mirror. The chip may be optimistic UI, but the parsed result is not durable state until the owning write path reconciles.

Correctness invariants:

- **Ambiguity cannot commit silently.** Low confidence, missing components, or multiple plausible anchors keep `needs_confirmation` true and require an explicit choice.
- **DST ranges are named-zone calculations.** A range that crosses a DST boundary is computed in the resolved IANA timezone, not by adding raw seconds.
- **All-day stays all-day.** Date-only parses are floating all-day values until the owning surface writes them; they are never coerced to midnight UTC in the parser.
- **Context-relative phrases need an accessible anchor.** If "before the board call" depends on a calendar event hidden by `resolve_access()` or the privacy ladder, the picker asks for clarification or declines instead of guessing.
- **Manual override clears AI provenance.** Once the operator edits the chip, the value becomes a plain user-authored parse with a fresh confirmation boundary.

## Build economics

The picker should earn its way into the app in the same order the calendar does: prove the cheap deterministic path, then spread it, then pay for AI and Availability only where the correction data says they help.

| Phase | Smallest useful scope | Evidence to expand |
| ----- | --------------------- | ------------------ |
| P1 | One inline field on `df_task` due date, chrono-only, offline, no network. | High parse success, low correction rate, keyboard and screen-reader flow verified. |
| P2 | All date inputs plus AI escalation for recurrence, context-relative, low-confidence, and unparsed phrases. | Escalation rate stays low, AI corrections improve accepted values, failures degrade to visual picker. |
| P3 | Voice, predictive defaults, and Availability-aware suggestions for multi-participant scheduling. | Availability calls are cached, suggestions beat blind defaults, and privacy-bounded explanations remain accurate. |

The force-all-AI flag is an evaluation and edge-case tool, not the default economics of the control. The default product should feel instant because chrono handles the common path locally.

## Open questions / risks

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="AI latency & token cost">
    Every escalation is a network round-trip and a token spend. The deterministic gate exists precisely to keep escalation rare (~10%); watch the escalation rate as a cost metric. Model-tier routing (cheap default → strong on low confidence) caps per-parse cost. If escalation creeps high, the gate's regexes/certainty thresholds are too eager — tune them, don't widen the model spend.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Debounce vs parse-on-commit">
    chrono is cheap enough to parse on a debounced keystroke (it drives the ghost-text echo). AI must **never** parse per keystroke — escalate only on blur / `Enter` / explicit commit, or the token cost and latency are unacceptable. The two tiers have different trigger cadences on purpose.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ambiguity & disambiguation UX">
    "Friday" forward or back? "next week" inclusive of this weekend? Low-confidence parses must disambiguate inline (tappable options) or via a one-turn Copilot clarification — never silently pick and never block the operator. `forwardDate: true` in chrono options handles the common "assume future" case for free.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Locale & multilingual">
    chrono has per-locale parsers but they're uneven; non-English and exotic formats are a natural AI-tier route (and a reason to expose the force-all-AI flag for some users). Spell out months in the echo to dodge `MM/DD` vs `DD/MM` ambiguity entirely.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Offline & degraded AI">
    When offline or when the Robert API (`Bd_dCiOz`) is unavailable, the component must degrade to chrono-only (the option C floor) gracefully — recurrence/context phrases then fail with an honest "couldn't parse, pick visually" rather than hanging on a dead network call. The hybrid already contains this floor by construction.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Accuracy & trust">
    The whole design rests on trust: the operator must believe the echoed time. That is why the Preview Chip is mandatory, the AI label is honest (reverts on override), and the "why this time" popover always explains. A single silently-wrong commit erodes trust faster than ten visible disambiguations. Log corrections (operator edited the chip) to tune the gate and the model-tier thresholds.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Over-triggering the AI">
    The biggest hybrid failure mode is the gate escalating phrases chrono could have handled — every false escalation is wasted latency and money. Bias the gate **toward chrono**: only escalate on a real recurrence/context hit or genuine component uncertainty, and measure the escalation rate against the correction rate to find the right threshold.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Timezone correctness">
    The classic bug: store wall-clock, lose the tz, render wrong elsewhere. Always store IANA + UTC, resolve from `user` (125) settings, show the tz on the chip, and do all math through `@date-fns/tz` `TZDate`. DST-spanning ranges must be computed in the named tz, never by adding seconds.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
