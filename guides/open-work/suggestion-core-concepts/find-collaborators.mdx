## Description

This outcome type surfaces people for project-level partnerships — research co-authors, content collaborators, technical contributors, creative partners. Not employment, not equity, not advisor: the relationship is bounded by a specific project or output.

**Best for:** researchers, creators, open-source maintainers, side-project builders, artists.

**Adjacent outcomes:** [Find a co-founder](/guides/open-work/suggestion-core-concepts/find-cofounder) when the project is becoming a company, [Hire key talent](/guides/open-work/suggestion-core-concepts/hire-key-talent) when the relationship is employment.

## Example Outcomes

Synthesized examples of what a User might create in the AI outcome chat for this type:

### Example 1 — Research paper co-author

<div style={{padding: '16px', border: '2px solid #6B9FE8', borderRadius: '8px', color: '#fff'}}>

PhD researcher in computational genomics at Stanford. Working on a paper combining ML with clinical genomics data — need a co-author who's a practicing oncologist with a research track record. Want someone who's published in JCO or NEJM at least once, and who can co-author the clinical interpretation sections credibly. US-based ideally.

</div>

### Example 2 — Podcast co-host

<div style={{padding: '16px', border: '2px solid #6B9FE8', borderRadius: '8px', color: '#fff'}}>

I host a podcast about scaling B2B SaaS companies, 40K downloads/episode. Looking for a co-host — but specifically not another podcaster. I want an actual operator (current or recent VP-level at a Series C+ company) who's a strong communicator and willing to commit to weekly recordings for 12 months.

</div>

### Example 3 — Open-source core contributor

<div style={{padding: '16px', border: '2px solid #6B9FE8', borderRadius: '8px', color: '#fff'}}>

Maintainer of a popular Python ORM (~8K stars). Need a TypeScript developer with database internals experience to lead a TypeScript port. Won't be paid initially but I'd hand over substantial maintainer access and we'd co-launch the port. Looking for someone who's already shipped a non-trivial OSS library, not someone looking to learn.

</div>
