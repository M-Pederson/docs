## The thesis

[Martin Tobias](https://incisive.vc) (Incisive Ventures, pre-seed) — [X, 2026-07-18](https://x.com/martingtobias/status/2078600868635820436):

> "Can you raise pre-seed with no MVP?" is the wrong question.
>
> The right one: **which investors fund at YOUR evidence stage?**
>
> A thread on the 2026 pre-seed evidence bar 👇

Round names are labels; what matters is the **evidence a company had actually demonstrated when the check was written**. Two seed rounds from our own tables make the point:

| Company | Label | Size | Public evidence at raise (verbatim from stored articles) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| /dev/agents | Seed | \$56M | "it is exceedingly rare for her firm to invest in a **pre-product** company"; "expects to have the first version of its product available by early-to-mid next year" |
| Chingari | Seed | \$1.3M | "about **25 million downloads**, roughly **3 million daily active users**, and a 30% ad-commission payout to creators" |

Same label — opposite ends of the evidence ladder. The **delta between round label and evidenced stage** is the per-round signal, and the per-investor _pattern_ of that delta is the product: an **evidence-stage narrative for every investor who writes pre-seed and seed checks** — the bar at which they actually fund, derived from what press coverage proves, not from what the firm says about itself. That is Tobias's "find investors who fund at your evidence stage" operationalized against 54k tracked early-stage rounds.

## Scope: two-layer (decided 2026-07-20)

**Layer 1 — evidence stage (early rounds only).** The E0–E5 ladder discriminates where evidence is murky: pre-seed, seed & Series A. The P1 run proved it saturates right on schedule — Series A already medians E4 — so Series B\+ gets **no ladder** (those raises are priced on KPIs and the stage would flatline at E4). Ladder population: `SEED` \+ `SERIES_A` \+ EQUITY/CONVERTIBLE\_NOTE rounds passing the early cut (**size ≤ ~\$15M AND the company's 1st or 2nd tracked financing** — decided; exact count needs the cut query).

**Layer 2 — investor rationale (ALL rounds).** The "what evidence drove the check" extraction is stage-agnostic and runs over the **entire 146k-deal corpus** — this is the general **investor-thesis narrative**: what evidence types (team / market / product / traction) an investor responds to, at any stage. Layer-2-only calls emit rationale \+ `claims_vs_metrics` with `evidence_stage` null-by-design.

Population, from sandbox:

| Slice | Layer | Rounds |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `financing_type = "SEED"` (Seed \+ Pre-seed; ~11% `pre=true`) | 1 \+ 2 | **28,761** |
| `financing_type = "SERIES_A"` (A \+ Pre-A) | 1 \+ 2 | **25,282** |
| EQUITY / CONVERTIBLE\_NOTE passing the early cut | 1 \+ 2 | TBD (cut query pending) |
| Everything else (Series B\+, growth EQUITY, debt, grants) | 2 only | remainder of **146,175** |

<Tip>
  **Two-tier corpus.** `deals.long_description` is already an article-derived synthesis — the /dev/agents one carries the pre-product admission verbatim. **Tier A:** classify from `long_description` alone (short, always present, near-free). **Tier B:** pull full windowed article text only when Tier A is low-confidence or signals conflict. Most rounds never need Tier B.
</Tip>

**The point-in-time trap (verified, load-bearing).** Sensate raised its \$3.2M seed in **April 2023**; its `canonical_deal_id` group holds **8 articles including an August 2024 press release** citing "\$17M in lifetime revenue" and "65M minutes of relaxation delivered." Articles are grouped per deal, _not_ filtered to the raise. Loop 1 must classify only articles inside **\[announce − 90d, announce \+ 14d\]** (both `articles.date` and `deals.date` exist; the /dev/agents articles land at \+0d and \+5d) — otherwise 2024 evidence leaks into a 2023 raise and the whole point-in-time premise collapses.

## The evidence ladder (rubric as config, not code)

**Decided 2026-07-20: v1 ships on the house E0–E5 ladder below.** Tobias's thread (only the first post is captured — X blocks anonymous thread fetch) merges later as `evidence_rubric v1.1`; bumping `rubric_version` reclassifies idempotently (a full 200-round rerun costs ~\$0.06). The P1 finding that **nothing scored E5** gets revisited at that merge.

| Stage | Bar | Typical press signals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **E0** — team & idea | Founders \+ thesis, nothing shipped | Pedigree bios, "pre-product", vision quotes |
| **E1** — product shipped | Live product, no external proof | "launches", "emerges from stealth", demo walkthroughs |
| **E2** — pilots & validation | Named pilots, design partners, LOIs, clinical/technical validation | "piloting with", "design partner", "tested in clinics" |
| **E3** — paying / named customers | Named customers or live revenue mechanics | Customer lists, pricing live, "customers include …" |
| **E4** — repeatability | Multiple logos, retention/usage/growth numbers | DAU/MAU, cohort counts, growth %, renewal mentions |
| **E5** — distribution proof | Scalable channel demonstrated | Channel economics, marketplace/partner distribution numbers |

Rules that ride with the ladder (also config):

1. A **named customer or a number** beats any adjective. "Used by leading brands" is a _claim_, not evidence — tracked via a `claims_vs_metrics` flag, never a stage bump.
2. **No articles ≠ E0.** A round with no usable press emits `insufficient_evidence`, not a stage. Absence of coverage is not absence of traction.
3. The output is the **publicly evidenced floor** — what the company had _provably_ achieved in press at raise, which is ≤ its actual stage.

## Loop 1 — Round evidence (classify every early-stage round)

One structured-output LLM call per `canonical_deal_id`. Layer-1 rounds get the full contract; Layer-2-only rounds (Series B\+ etc.) run the same call with the ladder disabled — rationale and `claims_vs_metrics` only.

**Input:** round label \+ `pre`, `size_usd`, `date`, `long_description`, and up to 2 raise-window articles (windowed \[−90d, \+14d\], `is_primary` first, `source_priority` ascending, ~4.5–5k chars each).

**Classifier system prompt v0 (verbatim — what the POC, bake-off, and rig all ran; the Go handoff artifact):**

```text
You classify the publicly evidenced traction stage of a startup at the moment of a specific funding round, using ONLY the provided deal description and press articles. You also extract what evidence investors said drove their decision to invest.

EVIDENCE LADDER (assign the highest stage with direct evidence in the texts):
E0 = team and idea only; nothing shipped. Signals: pedigree bios, pre-product, vision quotes.
E1 = product shipped or demoable; no external proof. Signals: launches, out of stealth, demo walkthroughs.
E2 = external validation without clear payment: named pilots, design partners, LOIs, clinical or technical validation, waitlists.
E3 = paying or named customers, or live revenue mechanics: customer lists, live pricing or subscriptions, first revenue.
E4 = repeatability: multiple logos, usage, retention or growth numbers such as downloads at scale, DAU or MAU, growth rates, revenue figures.
E5 = distribution proof: a scalable acquisition or distribution channel demonstrably working with numbers.

RULES:
1. POINT IN TIME: judge only what was demonstrably true at the round date per the texts. Anything described as a plan, expectation or upcoming release is NOT evidence.
2. A named customer or a hard number beats any adjective. Phrases like used by leading brands are claims, not evidence.
3. If the material contains no usable traction evidence, set insufficient_evidence to true and evidence_stage to null.
4. The stage is the publicly evidenced FLOOR of what the company achieved, not its actual private stage.
5. investor_rationale: include only statements explicitly attributed to a named investor or firm about why they invested. Tag each with evidence_type: team, market, traction, product or other.

Return ONLY valid JSON with exactly these keys, no markdown fences, no commentary:
{"evidence_stage": "E0|E1|E2|E3|E4|E5 or null", "confidence": "high|medium|low", "insufficient_evidence": false, "company_evidence": [{"type": "product_status|customers|usage|revenue|validation|team", "quote": "verbatim from text", "stage_implied": "E0..E5"}], "investor_rationale": [{"investor": "name or firm", "evidence_type": "team|market|traction|product|other", "quote": "verbatim"}], "what_drove_check": "one sentence on what evidence drove investors", "claims_vs_metrics": "metrics|claims_only|none"}
```

The user message carries: `ROUND: <label> | company: <name> | size_usd_millions: <n> | announce_date: <iso>`, then `DEAL DESCRIPTION:`, then `PRESS ARTICLES IN RAISE WINDOW (n of m total for this deal):` with the windowed article text.

**Worked example — /dev/agents \$56M seed → E0 high** (signals: "exceedingly rare … pre-product company", "first version … early-to-mid next year", team-pedigree context); Chingari \$1.3M seed → **E3–E4** (25M downloads, 3M DAU). Same label, four stages apart — that spread is what Loop 2 aggregates.

**Idempotency (production spec):** keyed `(canonical_deal_id, rubric_version)` — reruns are no-ops, rubric bumps reclassify cleanly.

## Loop 2 — Investor evidence profile (aggregate, no LLM)

Join classified rounds to investors: `fundable_organizations (is_investor)` ↔ `fundable_institutional_investments` **and** angel persons via `fundable_angel_investments` (angels in v1 — decided). Minimum **N = 5** classified early-stage rounds for a profile; `insufficient_evidence` rounds don't count toward N but are reported as coverage (decided).

Pure aggregation per investor: stage distribution \+ **median evidence stage**; `pre_evidence_pct` (≤ E1); `proof_pct` (≥ E3); lead vs follow split; **era-bucketed** (pre-2020 / 2020–22 / 2023\+ \+ all-time — decided); `insufficient_evidence` coverage; and from Layer 2, the investor's **rationale fingerprint** (team/market/product/traction mix, any stage).

<Warning>
  **Attribution guard (found in testing, verified working in the rig).** Incisive Ventures' investment list includes a \$20M pharma convertible note actually led by "**Incisive Capital**" — a same-name misjoin upstream. Guard: a round counts toward a profile only if the investor's name appears in that round's article or description text. The rig's first Incisive run correctly excluded 2 of 16 rounds on this guard. If the guard would exclude every round (thin-press angels), it bypasses with a flag and the narrative carries an attribution caution.
</Warning>

## Loop 3 — Narrative (per investor, LLM)

Low-volume, user-facing generation: profile \+ top-K signal quotes in, **2–4 sentences \+ a "Revealed evidence bar:" line \+ receipts** out.

**Narrative system prompt v0 (verbatim — copy-paste; the user message is the JSON payload of investor, profile, receipts, rounds):**

```text
You write a short evidence-stage narrative about ONE investor, grounded ONLY in the provided profile JSON and receipts.
RULES:
1. Two to four sentences, then a final sentence starting with: Revealed evidence bar:
2. Every number must come from a profile field. Never invent counts, stages or companies.
3. Every qualitative claim must trace to a receipt; cite companies by name.
4. Start with a bold markdown claim about the bar this investor funds at.
5. If profile.n_staged is below 5, state plainly that coverage is thin.
6. If profile.guard_bypassed is true, add a caution that investor attribution could not be text-verified.
Return ONLY valid JSON, no markdown fences: {"narrative": "text with markdown bold", "receipts_used": [{"company": "name", "quote": "short quote"}]}
```

**Prompt v0.1 fix list (from the 7-model audition):** forbid internal plumbing in prose (models mentioned "guard bypass" and `n_staged`); require the bar line to be a **stage or stage-anchored phrase, never a count** (flash-lite ended with "Revealed evidence bar: 14 staged investments"); numbers must be copied field-for-field (qwen3-235b conflated "14 classified" with "14 at E4"); allow up to 5 sentences when a receipt earns it (kimi's best output ran long); raise `max_tokens` well above 900 for reasoning-mandatory models (muse-spark spent the whole budget on reasoning and returned an empty narrative).

**Surfaces (decided): investor profiles first** — narrative \+ expandable receipts, How-I-Know style; Discover cites it after; stage-alignment feature for the [Intelligence Engine](/guides/open-work/intelligence-engine) six-family scoring; [Deal Flow](/guides/open-work/roadmap/deal-flow) investor context.

## P1 POC results (2026-07-20, sandbox)

The Loop-1 classifier ran same-day over **200 stratified rounds** — 67 pre-seed, 86 seed, 39 Series A, plus the Pre-A/EQUITY/convertible-note rows in Incisive's fixed set — **200/200 classified, zero failures**. Setup: `google/gemini-3.1-flash-lite`, reasoning off, temperature 0, deal `long_description` \+ up to 2 raise-window articles, `rubric_version=v0-house`, `run_tag=poc-v0`. Rows live in `evidence_stage_poc` (#765, sandbox).

**Stage distribution by label** — the within-label spread is the product:

| Label | n | E0 | E1 | E2 | E3 | E4 | insufficient |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pre-seed | 67 | 13 | 18 | 9 | 13 | 13 | 1 |
| Seed | 86 | 16 | 18 | 13 | 15 | 24 | 0 |
| Series A | 39 | 1 | 3 | 3 | 9 | 23 | 0 |

19% of seeds were funded at E0 while 39% of pre-seeds already showed E3\+ proof — same labels, opposite evidence. Series A concentrates at E4, confirming the scope rule while exposing a 4-round conviction tail at ≤E1. Nothing scored E5.

**What drove the checks:** **115/200 rounds** carried at least one explicitly investor-attributed rationale quote — 203 quotes typed **market 67 · product 59 · team 44 · traction 23 · other 10**. Early-stage investors publicly justify checks with market and product far more than traction — which makes demonstrated-traction funding (the E3/E4 cohort) the sharpest narrative differentiator an investor can own.

**Quality stats.** `insufficient_evidence` 1/200; `claims_vs_metrics`: **claims\_only 119 / metrics 76 / none 5** — ~60% of early-stage funding press has zero hard numbers. Confidence "high" on 198/200 — flash-tier over-confidence, a P2 calibration target. (Sample drawn from article-bearing id-order pages; 0/200 windowless rounds is not a corpus-wide coverage estimate.)

**Incisive Ventures — real Loop-2 preview** (all 16 rounds classified): **median E4, 13/16 at ≥E3, zero E0** — receipts: TurnStay (ZAR 250M processed), Moddule (6x revenue growth), Cuéntame (64K users / 30 named enterprises), Spare8 (30K downloads in four weeks), PlayMaker (five named clients).

**Delta highlights:** _capital ahead of proof_ (seeds at E0, by size): /dev/agents \$56M, Carmoola \$35.2M, Fractile \$15.4M, Next Insurance \$13M, 1001 AI \$9M — every rationale team pedigree \+ market size. _Outperformer flags_ (pre-seeds at E4): Vint, Cuéntame, Moddule, Ark Climate (43 German municipalities), Crink. _Conviction As_ (≤E1): Protego Biopharma \$51M, Promise \$20M, Odyssey \$18M.

**Cost actuals:** avg 1,776 in / 249 out tokens per round → the 200-round run ≈ **\$0.06**; a full 54k backfill extrapolates to **~\$15–25**.

**Reading the matrix.** The premium models agree with the flash-lite baseline far more (80–86% exact) than the cheap alternates do (60–65%) — flash-lite anchors the consensus cluster, while qwen3-235b compresses toward E1 and deepseek dissolves E0. Adjacent-agreement ≥92% everywhere says the ladder itself is robust; the 9–14 off-by-2 deals per model are the **P2 hand-label worklist**, which settles correctness (not just consistency) between flash-lite and qwen3.7-plus. Caveat found mid-audition: identical temp-0 reruns shift a stage on the margin (Incisive's E4 count moved 7↔8 between runs) — provider nondeterminism; another reason P2's eval gate precedes any backfill.

**Winning narrative (kimi-k3, v0 prompt, verbatim):**

> **Incisive Ventures funds at the E4 bar — companies with live products and measurable commercial traction.** Across 14 staged rounds, 8 sit at E4, with proof-bearing evidence in 86% of cases and a median stage of E4 in both the 2020–22 and 2023\+ eras. The receipts show what that bar looks like in practice: ShoppinPal's API platform serving 10,000 customers and partners, Katalyst shipping over 1,200 units through its Early Access program, and Moddule growing revenue 6x since September 2024. The fund almost never leads (7% lead rate) and its rationale fingerprint skews toward market (6) and product (5) signals over team (1), consistent with a follow-on investor underwriting demonstrated traction rather than founding narrative. Revealed evidence bar: a shipped product with quantified adoption or revenue, typically at seed or Series A scale.

**Value pick (qwen3.7-plus, same prompt):**

> **Incisive Ventures funds at the E4 evidence bar**, with 8 of its 14 staged investments reaching this median stage. The firm backs companies demonstrating tangible traction, such as Spare8 with over 30K downloads and Shoppinpal serving 10,000 customers. Recent activity remains concentrated at E4, including Moddule which reported 6x revenue growth since September 2024. Revealed evidence bar: E4

**Recommendation (Claude, 2026-07-20):**

- **Narrative model: `moonshotai/kimi-k3`**, with `qwen/qwen3.7-plus` as the 429-fallback. Loop 3 is low-volume (investors clearing N ≥ 5 — a few thousand narratives ≈ \$30–70 total at kimi prices), so the premium model is affordable for the user-facing artifact.
- **Bulk classifier: `google/gemini-3.1-flash-lite` or `qwen/qwen3.7-plus`** — P2 hand-labels decide on correctness. **Escalation tier: kimi-k3** on low-confidence / conflicting rounds (~10–15%).
- **Disqualified:** qwen3-235b (degeneration on this payload), muse-spark (reasoning-mandatory \+ 24% failure rate), fusion (integration \+ pricing opacity).

**Integration findings for the Go team:** muse-class models reject `reasoning: {enabled: false}` (mode must be configurable); reasoning models need narrative `max_tokens` far above 900 or they return empty; Xano text-input defaults need `first_notempty` (omitted text arrives as `""`, not null — this bug cost the first smoke run).

## Eval & guardrails

- **P2: hand-label ~120 rounds** seeded by the bake-off disagreement worklist (9–14 off-by-2 deals per model): exact \+ adjacent agreement, per-stage confusion matrix, **confidence calibration** (198/200 "high" is not credible), **quote fidelity** (verbatim-ness of extracted signals). Gate: ≥ 85% adjacent agreement before any backfill.
- Standing guards: point-in-time window; syndicate dedupe; claims-vs-metrics; `insufficient_evidence` ≠ E0; attribution guard (with bypass-flag fallback); floor semantics.
