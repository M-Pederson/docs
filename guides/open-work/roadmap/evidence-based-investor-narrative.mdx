## What we're building

Per [Martin Tobias](https://x.com/martingtobias/status/2078600868635820436) (Incisive Ventures): round names are labels — the real question is **which investors fund at your evidence stage**. From our own tables:

| Company | Label | Size | Public evidence at raise |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| /dev/agents | Seed | \$56M | "pre-product"; first release "early-to-mid next year" → **E0** |
| Chingari | Seed | \$1.3M | 25M downloads, 3M DAU → **E3–E4** |

Same label, opposite evidence. We classify the **publicly evidenced stage** of every early-stage round from press at raise, aggregate per investor, and generate a **receipt-cited evidence narrative** — the bar an investor actually funds at, with quotes as receipts. Surfaces: investor profiles first (How-I-Know style), then Discover, [Intelligence Engine](/guides/open-work/intelligence-engine) stage-alignment, [Deal Flow](/guides/open-work/roadmap/deal-flow).

## Fundable data contract (sandbox-verified 2026-07-20)

**Stage-eligible population:** SEED **28,761** \+ SERIES\_A **25,282** \+ EQUITY/CONVERTIBLE\_NOTE passing the early cut (**size ≤ ~\$15M AND the company's 1st/2nd tracked financing**; exact count TBD) ≈ **54k of 146,175 deals**. Everything else gets rationale-only extraction (stage null by design). All model picks are settled — bulk classifier `google/gemini-3.1-flash-lite` (kimi-referee eval, below), narrative `moonshotai/kimi-k3` with `qwen/qwen3.7-plus` failover.

| Table | Used for |
| --- | --- |
| `fundable_deals` (671) | Round meta: `financing_type`, `pre`, `size_usd`, `date`, `canonical_deal_id`, `long_description` (article-derived synthesis, 44/44 populated) |
| `fundable_articles` (680) | **Full article text** \+ `date`, `source_priority`, `is_primary`, keyed by `canonical_deal_id` (394,076 rows; early-stage deals carry 2–8 articles) |
| `fundable_institutional_investments` (678) / `fundable_angel_investments` (679) | Investor org / person ↔ deal \+ `lead_investor` |
| `fundable_organizations` (666) / `fundable_people` (667) | Investor entities (`is_investor`, `investment_stage`, `name`) |

Three verified gotchas the implementation must honor:

1. **`pre` is a generic "Pre-X" modifier** (a `SERIES_C, pre=true` row is a Pre-C round). Pre-seed = `SEED + pre=true`; there is no `PRE_SEED` enum.
2. **Point-in-time window is mandatory:** articles group per deal, not per raise — Sensate's Apr-2023 seed group contains an Aug-2024 PR citing "\$17M lifetime revenue." Classify only articles in **\[announce − 90d, \+14d\]**, `is_primary` first, `source_priority` ascending, ≤2 articles, ~5k chars each.
3. **Same-name investor misjoins exist** (Incisive Ventures' list contains an "Incisive Capital" deal) — the attribution guard below is required, not optional.

## Pipeline spec

### Step 1 — Classify (one LLM call per round)

Input: round label \+ `pre`, `size_usd`, `date`, `long_description`, windowed articles. **Model: `google/gemini-3.1-flash-lite`** (picked 2026-07-20 via the kimi-referee eval — 77% exact / 94% adjacent / bias −0.06 over 120 referee-labeled rounds; the ≥85%-adjacent gate passed), `reasoning: {enabled: false}`, temperature 0. **Escalate to `moonshotai/kimi-k3` on objective triggers only** — zero windowed articles, `claims_only` on a \$10M\+ round, parse/validation retries — never on self-reported confidence (measured unusable: 198/200 "high"). Early-stage rounds emit the full contract; later rounds run stage-suppressed (rationale \+ `claims_vs_metrics` only). Idempotent on `(canonical_deal_id, rubric_version)`.

**The evidence ladder (config, not code):** E0 team & idea · E1 product shipped · E2 pilots & validation · E3 paying/named customers · E4 repeatability (logos, usage, growth numbers) · E5 distribution proof. Riding rules: a named customer or hard number beats any adjective ("used by leading brands" is a claim → `claims_vs_metrics`); no articles ≠ E0 → `insufficient_evidence`; output is the **publicly evidenced floor**. (P1 note: nothing scored E5 in 200 rounds — revisit the E5 bar at the Tobias-thread merge.)

**System prompt v0 (verbatim — the handoff artifact):**

```text
You classify the publicly evidenced traction stage of a startup at the moment of a specific funding round, using ONLY the provided deal description and press articles. You also extract what evidence investors said drove their decision to invest.

EVIDENCE LADDER (assign the highest stage with direct evidence in the texts):
E0 = team and idea only; nothing shipped. Signals: pedigree bios, pre-product, vision quotes.
E1 = product shipped or demoable; no external proof. Signals: launches, out of stealth, demo walkthroughs.
E2 = external validation without clear payment: named pilots, design partners, LOIs, clinical or technical validation, waitlists.
E3 = paying or named customers, or live revenue mechanics: customer lists, live pricing or subscriptions, first revenue.
E4 = repeatability: multiple logos, usage, retention or growth numbers such as downloads at scale, DAU or MAU, growth rates, revenue figures.
E5 = distribution proof: a scalable acquisition or distribution channel demonstrably working with numbers.

RULES:
1. POINT IN TIME: judge only what was demonstrably true at the round date per the texts. Anything described as a plan, expectation or upcoming release is NOT evidence.
2. A named customer or a hard number beats any adjective. Phrases like used by leading brands are claims, not evidence.
3. If the material contains no usable traction evidence, set insufficient_evidence to true and evidence_stage to null.
4. The stage is the publicly evidenced FLOOR of what the company achieved, not its actual private stage.
5. investor_rationale: include only statements explicitly attributed to a named investor or firm about why they invested. Tag each with evidence_type: team, market, traction, product or other.

Return ONLY valid JSON with exactly these keys, no markdown fences, no commentary:
{"evidence_stage": "E0|E1|E2|E3|E4|E5 or null", "confidence": "high|medium|low", "insufficient_evidence": false, "company_evidence": [{"type": "product_status|customers|usage|revenue|validation|team", "quote": "verbatim from text", "stage_implied": "E0..E5"}], "investor_rationale": [{"investor": "name or firm", "evidence_type": "team|market|traction|product|other", "quote": "verbatim"}], "what_drove_check": "one sentence on what evidence drove investors", "claims_vs_metrics": "metrics|claims_only|none"}
```

User message shape: `ROUND: <label> | company: <name> | size_usd_millions: <n> | announce_date: <iso>` \+ `DEAL DESCRIPTION:` \+ `PRESS ARTICLES IN RAISE WINDOW (n of m):`.

### Step 2 — Profile (pure SQL, no LLM)

**No round cap in production.** Classification is corpus-wide and idempotent (Step 1 runs once per round, not per investor), so profiles aggregate over an investor's **entire classified book** — a16z's 1,568 tracked investments cost nothing extra at profile time, and era buckets measure real drift from the full history. The only cap is on the **narrative prompt payload** (Step 3): full-book profile stats \+ top-6 receipts \+ a most-recent-~30 rounds digest keep the prompt ~3k tokens regardless of book size.

Join classified rounds → investors (orgs \+ angels). **Attribution guard:** a round counts only if the investor matches that round's `attributed_investors` (precomputed at classify time — names found verbatim in the round text); excluded rounds → `guard_excluded`; if the guard empties an investor, keep all with `guard_bypassed` flagged. **Alias sets required (found in the a16z-at-64 rig test; implemented in rig v0.7 and verified same day):** press wrote "a16z" while the entity is "Andreessen Horowitz", falsely excluding 15/64 rounds — match against an alias set per investor (legal name, common short forms, domain stem like `a16z` from `a16z.com`, social handle), not the display name alone. Verification rerun: **false exclusions 15 → 0**, with exactly 15 rounds matching via the `a16z` domain-stem alias (`attribution_via` recorded per round). The rig also now mirrors production's stage-conditional ladder (later-stage rounds keep rationale, no stage), so rig profiles are true early-stage bars — a16z at 64: median E3 over 43 early rounds, 30% pre-evidence, 51% proof, 50% lead, with 20 later-stage rounds held out. Require N ≥ 5 staged rounds. Compute: stage distribution, median, `pre_evidence_pct` (≤E1), `proof_pct` (≥E3), `lead_pct`, era buckets (pre-2020 / 2020–22 / 2023\+ / all-time), `insufficient` coverage, rationale fingerprint (team/market/product/traction counts across all their rounds, any stage).

### Step 3 — Narrative (one LLM call per investor)

Fires on material profile change (median shift ≥ 1 stage or ≥ 20% new rounds). **Model contract (decided 2026-07-20):** kimi-k3 primary → qwen3.7-plus only on call failure; record the producer. Temp 0.2, `max_tokens` ≥ 900, reasoning off.

<Warning>
  **The narrative text is vectorized — stage codes are banned from prose.** "E4" is semantically empty in embedding space; a query like "investors who back proven revenue traction" can never match it. The prompt carries a stage-meanings glossary and the model writes the _meaning_ ("repeatable traction — multiple customers, usage at scale, revenue growth"); codes stay in the structured profile/receipts fields for badges and filters.
</Warning>

**Output validation (enforced, 2 attempts per model before failover):** must JSON-parse, must contain a "Revealed evidence bar:" line, must contain **no E0–E5 codes** (regex check). Both failure modes were observed live (a v0 kimi run dropped the bar line).

**System prompt v0.1 (verbatim — shipped in rig v0.5; user message = JSON of investor, profile, receipts, rounds):**

```text
You write a short evidence-stage narrative about ONE investor, grounded ONLY in the provided profile JSON and receipts.

STAGE MEANINGS (the profile and receipts use codes E0 through E5; your prose must use the meanings, NEVER the codes):
E0 = backing team and idea only, before any product exists
E1 = a shipped or demoable product with no external proof yet
E2 = external validation without clear payment: pilots, design partners, letters of intent, clinical or technical validation
E3 = paying or named customers and first revenue
E4 = repeatable traction: multiple customers or logos, usage at scale, retention or revenue growth numbers
E5 = a proven, scalable distribution channel

RULES:
1. Two to five sentences, then a final sentence starting with: Revealed evidence bar: followed by the meaning of the bar in plain language - never a code, never a count.
2. Never write the codes E0, E1, E2, E3, E4 or E5 anywhere in the narrative. This text is embedded for semantic search, so meaning-bearing words matter: write demonstrated traction, paying customers, pre-product, and so on.
3. Every number must be copied field-for-field from a profile field. Never invent or conflate counts: n_classified is the count of classified rounds; distribution values are per-stage counts; do not mix them.
4. Every qualitative claim must trace to a receipt; cite companies by name and keep their numbers verbatim.
5. Start with a bold markdown claim about the evidence bar this investor funds at, in plain language.
6. Never mention internal mechanics: guards, bypasses, field names, JSON, coverage plumbing.
7. If profile.n_staged is below 5, state plainly that coverage is thin.
8. If profile.guard_bypassed is true, add one caution sentence that the link between this investor and some rounds could not be verified in the source text.
Return ONLY valid JSON, no markdown fences: {"narrative": "text with markdown bold", "receipts_used": [{"company": "name", "quote": "short quote"}]}
```

## Go developer deployment (Universe backend)

Premise: **investor thesis already runs in Go on AlloyDB in Orbiter Universe**, publishing to Orbiter Backend via the [projection pattern](/guides/open-work/orbiter-univers-standalone/projection-pattern). The evidence narrative deploys as a **sibling of the thesis feature** — same investor entities, same projection channel.

### 1. Add

The three tables above, anchored to your entities: profile/narrative investor ref = **the Universe investor entity id the thesis feature keys on**, plus the source `fundable_organizations.id` / `fundable_people.id` for lineage. Vectorize `narrative` the same way thesis text is embedded so both retrieve side by side.

### 2. Change (minimal, additive)

- **Thesis surface:** no schema change — join `investor_evidence_narrative` by investor entity id at read time (or add a nullable `evidence_narrative_id` if you denormalize).
- **Projection package:** additive optional `side_panel_facts.evidence_narrative` section for investor-type entities — `{ narrative, receipts, median_stage, era_medians, proof_pct, coverage: {n_classified, n_insufficient, guard_excluded}, model, generated_at }`. Backend applier upserts it as a Universe-owned section (replace mode), never touching App-owned sections.
- **`company_display` / `person_display`:** optionally surface `evidence_bar` (e.g. "E3–E4") in `badges`; no new columns.
- **Universe graph (optional):** mirror `evidence_bar_current` onto investor nodes for stage-aligned graph matching.

### 3. Workers (the three steps)

1. **`evidence-classify`** — every round; ladder only for early-stage rounds; Step-1 prompt verbatim; idempotent on `(canonical_deal_id, rubric_version)`; precompute `attributed_investors`. **Trigger topology (clarified 2026-07-20): ROUND-driven, never investor-driven** — fires when a round enters the system (backfill, then the Fundable changelog flow); the PK is the already-ran check; one classification serves the whole syndicate. Investor enrichment never classifies — Step 2 aggregates what exists, reports gaps as coverage, and enqueues missing rounds for non-blocking background catch-up. **Late-article recheck:** if the first pass finds zero windowed articles, re-run once after the raise window closes (~announce \+15d) and upsert; after that only a `rubric_version` bump re-touches a round.
2. **`evidence-profile`** — Step-2 SQL with the guard and N ≥ 5; recompute on new classified rounds.
3. **`narrative-generate`** — Step-3 with the kimi→qwen failover contract and output validation (JSON parse \+ bar line \+ **no E0–E5 codes in prose** — the text is embedded); regenerate only on material profile change.

### 4. Config, not code

Rubric, both prompts, model ids, thresholds (N, window days, size cut, regen deltas) live in config; every row stamps `rubric_version` \+ `prompt_version` \+ `model`. A version bump = idempotent reclassify/regenerate, never a migration.

### 5. Rollout order

1. Wait for **P2 freeze** (bulk-classifier pick, prompt v0.1, eval gate ≥ 85% adjacent) — do not backfill on v0.
2. Migrate the three tables; wire config.
3. Early-stage ladder backfill (~54k rounds ≈ \$15–25 at flash rates; batch, cursor-resumable).
4. Rationale sweep over the remaining ~92k stage-suppressed rounds (same order of cost).
5. Profiles (N ≥ 5, orgs \+ angels) → narratives (≈ \$30–70 at kimi prices).
6. Projection wiring → investor-profile surface; hook new-deal classification into the Fundable changelog flow for continuous refresh.

### 6. Acceptance & observability

- **Acceptance:** run Incisive Ventures \+ 4 investors and diff against the rig's stored runs (`investor_narrative_poc`, tags `rig-v04-smoke` / `aud-*`). Stages may drift ±1 on the margin (provider nondeterminism — measured); medians, guard exclusions, and receipt fidelity must match.
- **Report per run:** coverage %, insufficient rate, guard exclusions, **kimi→qwen failover rate** (the kimi capacity-health signal — if it climbs, revisit the narrative-model choice), narrative validation failures, token spend by model.

## Validation (what's already proven, 2026-07-20)

**P1 POC — 200 stratified rounds, 200/200 classified, ≈ \$0.06** (flash-lite, sandbox). Stage spread by label (the product signal): pre-seed and seed each span E0→E4 (19% of seeds funded at E0; 39% of pre-seeds already ≥E3); Series A concentrates at E4 with a 4-round ≤E1 conviction tail. Rationale extraction: 115/200 rounds carried investor-attributed quotes — **market 67 · product 59 · team 44 · traction 23** — investors talk market/product far more than traction, making demonstrated-traction funding the sharpest differentiator. 60% of early-stage press is claims-only (zero hard numbers). Full 54k ladder backfill extrapolates to ~\$15–25.

**7-model bake-off** (same data, same prompts; ladder agreement vs the flash-lite baseline):

| Model | \$/M in/out | Exact / adj ±1 | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `moonshotai/kimi-k3` | 3.00 / 15.00 | **80% / 100%** (50) | **Narrative winner** — only model with synthesis, zero number errors (2/50 429s) |
| `qwen/qwen3.7-plus` | 0.32 / 1.28 | 74% / 95% (200) | Failover \+ bulk-classifier candidate — best value, spec-perfect narrative |
| `google/gemini-3.1-flash-lite` | 0.25 / 1.50 | baseline (200) | Bulk-classifier candidate; weak prose discipline |
