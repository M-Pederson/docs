<Note>
  This page explores and scopes moving the Orbiter Universe FalkorDB graph and
  Enrichment Engine into a separate AlloyDB-backed instance/deployment boundary
  with dedicated hardware. The goal is to understand isolation, throughput,
  scaling, migration, and operational cost before committing to a standalone
  production architecture.
</Note>

<Tip>
  **Terminology for this page:** **Universe backend** means the standalone
  enrichment service and its AlloyDB storage. **Universe graph** means the
  dedicated FalkorDB graph owned by that backend. **App backend** means the
  Orbiter product backend and its AlloyDB storage. **Product projections** are
  App backend read-model/card tables populated from Universe packages, not
  source-of-truth enrichment tables.
</Tip>

Use this page to collect scope, architecture, decisions, and implementation notes for the standalone Orbiter Universe workstream.

## Overview:

The current enrichment waterfalls already behave like a separate product inside Orbiter: they crawl public sources, run bounded cascades, write global graph nodes and edges, maintain source-specific queues, and materialize canonical people, companies, music entities, film/TV entities, funding rounds, investors, partners, and expertise. The strongest target architecture is to make that separation explicit.

At a high level:

- **App backend** owns the Orbiter product surface: users, organizations, teams, app workflows, auth, contacts, notes, and product-facing `master_*` records.
- **Universe backend** owns public-source enrichment state, Fundable mirrors, queue state, raw provider payloads, source freshness, and the control tables needed to run enrichment safely.
- **Orbiter Universe FalkorDB** is a standalone global public graph. It owns the canonical Universe graph: public people, companies, funding rounds, MusicBrainz entities, IMDB / film-TV entities, expertise nodes, source-derived edges, and global priors.
- **User Graphs and Organization Graphs** communicate with the Universe graph through explicit APIs, references, or graph-gateway reads. They should not directly mutate the Universe graph.

The App backend can still create and read `master_person`, `master_company`, `master_link`, `master_email`, `master_phone`, `master_avatar`, and related product records. The difference is that enrichment-derived values come from a call to the Universe backend instead of being created by the same runtime that stores product state.

### Why split this out

The enrichment waterfalls have different hardware, data, and failure characteristics than the app backend:

- **Dedicated hardware for graph-heavy work.** Universe enrichment writes and queries a dense global graph. Funding-round fanout, IMDB credit edges, MusicBrainz graph expansion, expertise resolution, and future priors/centrality jobs should not compete with product API traffic.
- **Different queue profile.** Person/company enrichment, IMDB, and MusicBrainz all have queue processors, retry surfaces, rate limits, depth caps, and seed-only expansion rules. These are background workloads, not interactive product workloads.
- **Cleaner data ownership.** Fundable tables serve the enrichment engine and should live only in the Universe backend. Product records should receive distilled results, not carry the raw source mirror.
- **Better blast-radius control.** A bad enrichment cascade, provider outage, scrape failure, or graph backfill should degrade enrichment, not the core Orbiter app.
- **Privacy boundary.** The Universe graph should hold public or source-verified data. User-private and organization-private context stays in the App backend and tenant graphs.
- **Future global intelligence.** Universe-level priors, centrality, similarity, and scoring baselines need a stable global graph that can be recomputed independently and then consumed by product, organization, and user layers.

### What the waterfall docs imply

The existing waterfall pages point toward the same boundary:

- **People** begin at `get-add/master-person-new`, dedup locally, call PDL / Enrich Layer / Exa, optionally match Fundable people, create `master_person` and related records, write a FalkorDB Person node, then dispatch `enrich-master-person`. That is currently both product record creation and global Universe expansion in one flow.
- **Companies** begin at `get-add/master-company-new`, run LLM / Serper enrichment, match Fundable organizations, create `master_company`, write company facts and links, create a FalkorDB Company node, then dispatch `enrich-master-company`. The downstream company orchestrator already gates seed-only expansion for Exa C-suite, YC, Fundable deal fanout, and funding-network participants.
- **MusicBrainz** is structurally a Universe workload. It has source tables, queue tables, a global 1 request/second rate limiter, seed entities, depth caps, MusicBrainz natural keys, and reconciliation bridges back to `master_person` / `master_company`.
- **IMDB** is also structurally a Universe workload. It has its own `imdb_person`, `film_tv`, `film_tv_award`, queue tables, scrape freshness, poster storage, credit-edge writers, award edges, and expertise extraction. The product only needs the useful person/title facts and graph connections.
- **Fundable** is the cleanest first ownership line. `fundable_organizations`, `fundable_people`, `fundable_deals`, `fundable_institutional_investments`, `fundable_institutional_investments_person`, `fundable_angel_investments`, `fundable_articles`, and future sync/changelog/graph-update tables should live only in the Universe backend.

### Ownership boundary

| Lives in App backend | Lives in Universe backend |
|---|---|
| `master_person`, `master_company`, `master_link`, `master_email`, `master_phone`, `master_avatar` | Fundable mirror tables and Fundable sync/changelog/update queues |
| User, organization, team, contact, note, app workflow, auth, permissions | Raw enrichment payloads and source-specific freshness state |
| Product-facing canonical rows and denormalized display/card fields | Person/company/music/IMDB enrichment queues and retry state |
| Product read models for film/TV, music, awards, and similar cards | Universe source-of-truth tables such as `mb_*`, `imdb_person`, `film_tv`, `film_tv_award` |
| User Graph and Organization Graph state | Universe graph source tables, source provenance, graph-write state, and graph replay metadata |
| References to Universe entities, such as `universe_person_uuid` / `universe_company_uuid` | Canonical Universe entity ids, graph node UUIDs, source provenance, and graph-write audit |

The App backend should not store Fundable source mirrors. It may store a reference to the Universe entity, a distilled funding summary, a confidence score, or product-specific display fields copied from a Universe response.

### Source tables vs card tables

The important refinement: some "source" tables also carry the data needed to render product cards. `imdb_person`, `film_tv`, `film_tv_award`, and the `mb_*` tables are not like raw Fundable mirrors. They describe user-visible entities: actors, creators, films, TV titles, awards, artists, labels, recordings, releases, events, and places.

So yes, these tables may need to exist in **both** AlloyDB environments, but they should not have the same responsibility.

| Table family | Universe backend role | App backend role |
|---|---|---|
| `imdb_person` | Source-of-truth IMDB enrichment row: raw scrape/LLM payload, freshness gates, cascade depth, graph node UUID, credit/award replay state, enrichment success. | Product read model for IMDB person cards and cross-links to `master_person`; stores display-safe fields, image, role summaries, `universe_person_uuid`, `universe_node_uuid`, `source_updated_at`, and projection sync metadata. |
| `film_tv` | Source-of-truth title row: raw IMDB title payload, poster processing, graph node UUID, credit-edge replay, award cascade state. | Product read model for Film/TV cards; stores title, year/date, type, poster, tagline/summary, key credits, `universe_film_tv_uuid`, `universe_node_uuid`, and projection freshness. |
| `film_tv_award` | Source-of-truth award row: raw award extraction, nominee/winner resolution state, graph edge/node write state. | Product read model for award badges, award sections, and title/person card modules; stores display-safe award text, year, category, status, linked product rows, and Universe references. |
| `mb_*` | Source-of-truth MusicBrainz rows: raw lookup/browse JSON, MBID natural keys, MusicBrainz rate-limit/freshness state, graph node UUIDs, cascade metadata, image/description generation state. | Product read models for music entity cards; stores display fields, canonical image URLs, short descriptions, MBIDs, Universe references, and links to `master_person` / `master_company` when reconciled. |

This is a projection pattern, not a dual-write source-of-truth pattern.

The Universe backend owns:

- raw provider payloads and source-specific payload history
- provider freshness gates, retry state, queue state, and cascade metadata
- graph node/edge mutation, graph replay, and graph-write audit
- provider natural keys such as MBID and IMDB URL as canonical source keys
- enrichment-only state that product screens should not depend on directly

The App backend owns:

- card-ready rows that are cheap to query in product flows
- denormalized display fields and product-specific labels
- references to product records such as `master_person_id` and `master_company_id`
- references back to Universe entities and graph nodes
- projection freshness fields, such as `source_updated_at` and `projection_synced_at`

Because the AlloyDB backend has not been built yet, use explicit projection names now instead of carrying the Xano-era ambiguity forward. The legacy Xano table names remain reference material for migration, but the AlloyDB design should make the boundary obvious in the schema:

| Universe source table | App backend product projection |
|---|---|
| `universe.imdb_person` | `app.imdb_person_card` |
| `universe.film_tv` | `app.film_tv_card` |
| `universe.film_tv_award` | `app.film_tv_award_card` |
| `universe.mb_artist`, `universe.mb_recording`, `universe.mb_label`, `universe.mb_event`, `universe.mb_release_group`, `universe.mb_release`, `universe.mb_work`, `universe.mb_place` | `app.music_entity_card` plus type-specific detail tables only when the UI needs them |

This gives product code clear read-model names while keeping Universe source tables close to the existing waterfall terminology.

<Warning>
Avoid bidirectional table sync for these rows. Universe should publish projection events or answer projection pulls; App backend should upsert its read models from those packages. Product edits should not mutate the Universe source row directly. If a user or org has a correction, send it through a proposed-correction flow and promote it to Universe only after public/source verification.
</Warning>

Likewise, Universe tables should avoid hard dependencies on product row IDs wherever possible. Instead of raw Fundable rows depending on `master_company_id` or `master_person_id`, the long-term shape should be:

```text
Fundable source row -> Universe entity UUID -> product master_* record reference
```

That keeps Fundable aligned with the global graph while letting the App backend decide how and when to create product records.

### Communication strategy

Use explicit service contracts rather than shared-table writes:

1. **Resolve APIs** answer "do we know this entity?" from public identifiers: LinkedIn URL, domain, email, phone, IMDB URL, MusicBrainz MBID, Fundable id, Crunchbase URL, and similar keys.
2. **Enrich APIs** run or queue Universe enrichment and return a canonical package: Universe UUID, graph node UUID, normalized identity, public links, safe contact facts, avatar/logo, source provenance, confidence, and freshness.
3. **Graph gateway APIs** let User Graphs and Organization Graphs read Universe paths, priors, and public context without writing to the Universe graph.
4. **Async update events** notify the App backend when a Universe entity improves. The App backend then decides whether to update `master_*` rows.
5. **Privacy-safe promotion** prevents private user/org facts from flowing into Universe unless they are independently verified by public/source data.

### Example: `get-add/master-person-new` today

At a high level, the current flow works like this:

1. A caller invokes `mvp/get-add/master-person-new` with links, emails, phones, name fields, `cascade_depth`, and priority metadata.
2. The function checks the person kill switch and runs product-local dedup against existing `master_link`, `master_email`, `master_phone`, and `master_person` data.
3. `cascade-person-data` gathers raw public enrichment from PDL, Enrich Layer, and Exa.
4. `match-master-person` tries to resolve an existing person from the enriched identifiers.
5. Fundable lookup runs from the enriched link set and may link `fundable_people` to the person.
6. If no existing person is found, the function creates or updates `master_person`, `master_link`, `master_email`, `master_phone`, avatar, and related product rows.
7. The same flow stores raw enrichment payloads and history rows.
8. The same flow calls `add-person-node` to create or update the Person node in FalkorDB.
9. The function dispatches `enrich-master-person` immediately for a depth-0 non-queued seed, or upserts `queue_enrich_person` for queued/deeper work.
10. The downstream person orchestrator writes more product rows, graph properties, edges, biographies, expertise, and related company queues.

The important observation: one entry point currently owns product dedup, source enrichment, Fundable matching, raw payload storage, graph mutation, queue routing, and product record creation.

### Example: `get-add/master-person-new` after separation

After the split, the same user-facing operation should keep the product contract but divide responsibility:

1. A caller asks the **App backend** to get or add a person.
2. The App backend first runs a cheap local dedup against `master_link`, `master_email`, `master_phone`, and `master_person`.
3. If the person already exists and is fresh enough, the App backend returns the local `master_person`.
4. If the person is missing or thin, the App backend calls the **Universe backend** with public identifiers and minimal context.
5. The Universe backend resolves or creates the canonical Universe Person, runs or queues the PDL / Enrich Layer / Exa / Fundable enrichment path, stores raw payloads in Universe AlloyDB, and writes the standalone Universe FalkorDB node/edges.
6. Fundable matching happens only inside the Universe backend. Fundable rows link to Universe entity UUIDs, not directly to product master rows.
7. The Universe backend returns a canonical person package: `universe_person_uuid`, `universe_node_uuid`, normalized name fields, public profile links, safe emails/phones if allowed, avatar, current role, source provenance, confidence, and freshness.
8. The App backend upserts `master_person`, `master_link`, `master_email`, `master_phone`, avatar, and product display fields from that package.
9. The App backend stores the Universe reference on the master record so future reads can ask the Universe graph for public context and paths.
10. The User Graph or Organization Graph links local private context to the product master record and/or Universe UUID by reference. Private notes, relationship strength, org-specific context, and user-specific edges stay outside the Universe graph.
11. If deeper enrichment finishes later, the Universe backend emits an update event. The App backend selectively refreshes local `master_*` fields from the new canonical package.

This keeps the product API stable while moving the expensive global work behind a dedicated enrichment boundary.

### Example: new person miss after separation

Suppose a user adds a person who does not exist in the App backend or the Universe backend yet:

```json
{
  "links": ["https://www.linkedin.com/in/example-person"],
  "full_name": "Example Person",
  "source": "user_add_contact"
}
```

Recommended default behavior:

<Steps>
  <Step title="App backend checks local identity first">
    It looks for existing `master_person`, `master_link`, `master_email`, and `master_phone` matches. Nothing is found.
  </Step>

  <Step title="App backend creates a usable local record quickly">
    It creates a thin `master_person` with the user's supplied name/link, writes the LinkedIn URL to `master_link`, marks the row as `enrichment_status = "pending"` or equivalent, and returns that product record to the caller.
  </Step>

  <Step title="App backend asks Universe backend to resolve/enrich">
    It sends public identifiers only: name, LinkedIn URL, email/phone if supplied, source context, and requested freshness/depth mode.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Universe backend creates the canonical Universe entity">
    It creates a Universe Person row and Universe graph node, then starts the enrichment waterfall: PDL / Enrich Layer / Exa, Fundable person matching, social/profile discovery, and downstream queue work as allowed by depth rules.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Universe backend returns an initial package if available">
    <Check>
      This is the fast enrichment handoff. If identity resolution completes inside the request budget, the App backend can immediately update the local `master_person` with `universe_entity_uuid`, `universe_node_uuid`, normalized name fields, avatar, headline, and public links.
    </Check>
  </Step>

  <Step title="App backend keeps the product experience responsive">
    The user sees the contact immediately. The UI can show "enriching" state instead of waiting for the full Universe waterfall.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Universe backend continues asynchronously">
    Deeper work such as work history, companies, funding/investor edges, IMDB/MusicBrainz bridges, expertise, and graph priors runs in Universe queues.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Universe publishes an update event">
    When the canonical package improves, the App backend consumes the event and updates the local `master_person`, related links/contact fields, product projections, and any cached graph snippets.
  </Step>

  <Step title="User/private context stays local">
    Notes, relationship labels, organization-specific context, and private corrections stay in the App backend/User Graph/Organization Graph unless explicitly promoted through a verified correction flow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Synchronous enrichment should be an opt-in mode, not the default. It is useful for admin QA, backfills, controlled seed jobs, and tests where the caller needs to block until Universe has completed a richer identity package. Normal product creates should prefer "thin record now, richer record shortly" because the Universe waterfall may hit provider latency, rate limits, queue backpressure, or graph-heavy fanout.

### Initial success shape

The first successful version should be deliberately boring:

- Keep `master_*` records product-owned.
- Move Fundable source tables and Fundable graph replay into Universe-owned storage.
- Keep product card/read-model tables for IMDB, Film/TV, MusicBrainz, and awards in the App backend, but make them projections from Universe packages.
- Make Universe entity UUIDs the bridge between raw source rows, the Universe graph, and product master records.
- Start with App backend calls to Universe resolve/enrich APIs; avoid direct cross-database reads from app code.
- Keep User Graph and Organization Graph writes local, but allow read-time calls to Universe graph context.
- Return useful partial results quickly, then update asynchronously when enrichment finishes.
- Treat the existing Xano waterfall functions as reference behavior while rebuilding the boundary in the new AlloyDB services.

### Working decisions

These are the current default decisions for the standalone design. Treat them as the baseline until a specific implementation pass proves one should change.

| # | Decision | Rationale |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | **App backend owns final product writes.** The Universe backend should not directly write App backend `master_*` rows. It returns canonical packages and emits update events; App backend decides when to upsert `master_person`, `master_company`, `master_link`, `master_email`, `master_phone`, card rows, and other product tables. | Keeps the product database as the authority for product state, permissions, user/org context, and backwards-compatible API behavior. It also prevents enrichment backfills from mutating product records unexpectedly. |
| 2 | **IMDB, Film/TV, award, and MusicBrainz tables exist in both backends when product screens need them.** In the Universe backend, they are enrichment source-of-truth tables. In the App backend, they are product read models / card tables projected from Universe packages. | These entities are not raw mirrors like Fundable; they are user-visible cards. The product needs cheap local reads, but enrichment needs raw payloads, queues, graph replay, and source freshness state. |
| 3 | **Use explicit product projection names in AlloyDB.** Since the AlloyDB backend is greenfield, product-side IMDB / Film-TV / MusicBrainz tables should be named as read models: `imdb_person_card`, `film_tv_card`, `film_tv_award_card`, and `music_entity_card` unless a later UI requirement needs a type-specific detail table. Universe keeps source-table names such as `imdb_person`, `film_tv`, `film_tv_award`, and `mb_*`. | Avoids importing Xano-era ambiguity into the new database. Product code reads card/projection tables; Universe services own source-of-truth enrichment tables. |
| 4 | **Use common Universe reference columns everywhere.** Product master rows and card rows should carry `universe_entity_uuid`, `universe_node_uuid`, `universe_entity_type` where needed, `source_key`, `source_updated_at`, `projection_synced_at`, and `projection_version`. Type-specific aliases such as `universe_person_uuid` or `universe_film_tv_uuid` are allowed only when a hot query truly needs them. | A common reference contract works across people, companies, Film/TV, music, awards, funding rounds, and future entity types. Type-specific aliases are query conveniences only; they must not become a second identity system. |
| 5 | **User Graphs and Organization Graphs use a hybrid read model.** They read stable/product-needed fields from App backend projections, ask a Universe graph gateway for live paths, priors, and broad public context, and materialize hot snippets into local caches only when needed. They do not write directly to Universe. | This gives product speed without copying the whole public graph into every tenant graph. The graph gateway preserves the privacy boundary and keeps Universe graph compute on dedicated hardware. |
| 6 | **Product creates should return fast, useful records and continue enrichment asynchronously.** The App backend should do local dedup, create or return a usable thin product record, call Universe for identity resolution / an initial canonical package, and let deeper waterfalls complete async. Specific admin/backfill/QA flows can request synchronous enrichment explicitly. | Keeps interactive product flows responsive while preserving a path for workflows that truly need a richer result before continuing. The default user experience is "thin record now, richer record shortly," which matches the bounded-cascade direction: seed identity first, deeper expansion later. |
| 7 | **Only copy display-safe public contact facts into product contact tables.** `master_email` / `master_phone` should receive public or provider-licensed facts with source, confidence, and freshness. Raw provider payloads, low-confidence inferred values, and enrichment-only contact evidence stay in Universe. | Product contact rows are used in user-facing workflows. They need provenance and conservative promotion rules; raw enrichment evidence should remain behind the Universe boundary. |
| 8 | **Private corrections flow through a proposed-correction path.** User/org edits should update product/private state immediately when appropriate, but they should not directly mutate Universe. Promote a correction to Universe only after public-source verification or explicit operator review. | Prevents private tenant data from leaking into the global graph and keeps the Universe graph aligned with public/source-verified truth. |
| 9 | **Fundable lives only in Universe.** Fundable mirror tables, sync runs, changelogs, and graph replay queues should not exist in App backend except as distilled fields or references on product projections. | Fundable serves the enrichment engine and funding graph. Product needs results, not the raw source mirror or graph replay machinery. |
| 10 | **Projection sync is one-way by default: Universe backend → App backend.** Universe publishes packages/events; App backend consumes them and updates read models. Product-origin changes become proposed corrections or private overlays, not direct source-table writes. | Avoids bidirectional sync ambiguity, circular updates, and hard-to-debug source-of-truth conflicts. |

### Current resolved posture

There are no unresolved architecture questions on this page at the scoping level. The next pass should turn these working decisions into an implementation plan: schemas, service contracts, sync events, migration order, and cutover strategy.
