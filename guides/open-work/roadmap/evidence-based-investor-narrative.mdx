## The thesis

[Martin Tobias](https://incisive.vc) (Incisive Ventures, pre-seed) — [X, 2026-07-18](https://x.com/martingtobias/status/2078600868635820436):

> "Can you raise pre-seed with no MVP?" is the wrong question.
>
> The right one: **which investors fund at YOUR evidence stage?**
>
> A thread on the 2026 pre-seed evidence bar 👇

Round names are labels; what matters is the **evidence a company had actually demonstrated when the check was written**. Two seed rounds from our own tables make the point:

| Company | Label | Size | Public evidence at raise (verbatim from stored articles) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| /dev/agents | Seed | \$56M | "it is exceedingly rare for her firm to invest in a **pre-product** company"; "expects to have the first version of its product available by early-to-mid next year" |
| Chingari | Seed | \$1.3M | "about **25 million downloads**, roughly **3 million daily active users**, and a 30% ad-commission payout to creators" |

Same label — opposite ends of the evidence ladder. The **delta between round label and evidenced stage** is the per-round signal, and the per-investor _pattern_ of that delta is the product: an **evidence-stage narrative for every investor who writes pre-seed and seed checks** — the bar at which they actually fund, derived from what press coverage proves, not from what the firm says about itself. That is Tobias's "find investors who fund at your evidence stage" operationalized against 54k tracked early-stage rounds.

## Scope: two-layer (decided 2026-07-20)

**Layer 1 — evidence stage (early rounds only).** The E0–E5 ladder discriminates where evidence is murky: pre-seed, seed & Series A. The P1 run proved it saturates right on schedule — Series A already medians E4 — so Series B\+ gets **no ladder** (those raises are priced on KPIs and the stage would flatline at E4). Ladder population: `SEED` \+ `SERIES_A` \+ EQUITY/CONVERTIBLE\_NOTE rounds passing the early cut (**size ≤ ~\$15M AND the company's 1st or 2nd tracked financing** — decided; exact count needs the cut query).

**Layer 2 — investor rationale (ALL rounds).** The "what evidence drove the check" extraction is stage-agnostic and runs over the **entire 146k-deal corpus** — this is the general **investor-thesis narrative**: what evidence types (team / market / product / traction) an investor responds to, at any stage. Layer-2-only calls emit rationale \+ `claims_vs_metrics` with `evidence_stage` null-by-design.

Population, from sandbox:

| Slice | Layer | Rounds |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `financing_type = "SEED"` (Seed \+ Pre-seed; ~11% `pre=true`) | 1 \+ 2 | **28,761** |
| `financing_type = "SERIES_A"` (A \+ Pre-A) | 1 \+ 2 | **25,282** |
| EQUITY / CONVERTIBLE\_NOTE passing the early cut | 1 \+ 2 | TBD (cut query pending) |
| Everything else (Series B\+, growth EQUITY, debt, grants) | 2 only | remainder of **146,175** |

<Note>
  **`pre` flag semantics (verified):** `pre` is a generic "Pre-X" modifier, not a pre-seed marker — a sampled `SERIES_C, pre=true` row is Triastek's \$20.4M **Pre-C** round. Pre-seed is `SEED + pre=true`. There is no `PRE_SEED` enum value (0 rows).
</Note>

| `fundable_articles` (680) | **Full article text** (not just links): `text`, `link`, `date`, `source_priority` (quality rank), `is_primary`, `canonical_deal_id` | 394,076 rows; sampled early-stage deals carried 2–8 full-text articles each (TechCrunch, BusinessWire, trade press) |

<Tip>
  **Two-tier corpus.** `deals.long_description` is already an article-derived synthesis — the /dev/agents one carries the pre-product admission verbatim. **Tier A:** classify from `long_description` alone (short, always present, near-free). **Tier B:** pull full windowed article text only when Tier A is low-confidence or signals conflict. Most rounds never need Tier B.
</Tip>

**The point-in-time trap (verified, load-bearing).** Sensate raised its \$3.2M seed in **April 2023**; its `canonical_deal_id` group holds **8 articles including an August 2024 press release** citing "\$17M in lifetime revenue" and "65M minutes of relaxation delivered." Articles are grouped per deal, _not_ filtered to the raise. Loop 1 must classify only articles inside **\[announce − 90d, announce \+ 14d\]** (both `articles.date` and `deals.date` exist; the /dev/agents articles land at \+0d and \+5d) — otherwise 2024 evidence leaks into a 2023 raise and the whole point-in-time premise collapses.

## The evidence ladder (rubric as config, not code)

**Decided 2026-07-20: v1 ships on the house E0–E5 ladder below.** Tobias's thread (only the first post is captured — X blocks anonymous thread fetch) merges later as `evidence_rubric v1.1`; bumping `rubric_version` reclassifies idempotently (a full 200-round rerun costs ~\$0.06). The P1 finding that **nothing scored E5** gets revisited at that merge.

| Stage | Bar | Typical press signals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **E0** — team & idea | Founders \+ thesis, nothing shipped | Pedigree bios, "pre-product", vision quotes |
| **E1** — product shipped | Live product, no external proof | "launches", "emerges from stealth", demo walkthroughs |
| **E2** — pilots & validation | Named pilots, design partners, LOIs, clinical/technical validation | "piloting with", "design partner", "tested in clinics" |
| **E3** — paying / named customers | Named customers or live revenue mechanics | Customer lists, pricing live, "customers include …" |
| **E4** — repeatability | Multiple logos, retention/usage/growth numbers | DAU/MAU, cohort counts, growth %, renewal mentions |
| **E5** — distribution proof | Scalable channel demonstrated | Channel economics, marketplace/partner distribution numbers |

Rules that ride with the ladder (also config):

1. A **named customer or a number** beats any adjective. "Used by leading brands" is a _claim_, not evidence — tracked via a `claims_vs_metrics` flag, never a stage bump.
2. **No articles ≠ E0.** A round with no usable press emits `insufficient_evidence`, not a stage. Absence of coverage is not absence of traction.
3. The output is the **publicly evidenced floor** — what the company had _provably_ achieved in press at raise, which is ≤ its actual stage.

## Loop 1 — Round evidence (classify every early-stage round)

One structured-output LLM call per `canonical_deal_id`. Layer-1 rounds get the full contract; Layer-2-only rounds (Series B\+ etc.) run the same call with the ladder disabled — rationale and `claims_vs_metrics` only.

**Input:** round label \+ `pre`, `size_usd`, `date`, participating investor names, `long_description`, and (Tier B) deduped windowed article text capped ~8k tokens. Funding press is heavily syndicated — near-duplicate wire copies are dropped first (`source_priority` ascending, `is_primary` first, minhash/embedding near-dup filter; expect 30–50% token savings).

**Output (worked example — real round, real quotes, sandbox article `abac5bff…`):**

```json
{
  "canonical_deal_id": "1886a4a9-da97-4ed6-8d24-7508bfdc36a5",
  "round": { "label": "SEED", "pre": false, "size_usd_m": 56, "date": "2024-11-28", "company": "/dev/agents" },
  "evidence_stage": "E0",
  "confidence": "high",
  "signals": [
    { "type": "product_status", "direction": "ceiling",
      "quote": "it is exceedingly rare for her firm to invest in a pre-product company",
      "article_id": "abac5bff-ab88-4b46-8139-aaeb6dab9ac3" },
    { "type": "roadmap_not_shipped", "direction": "ceiling",
      "quote": "The company expects to have the first version of its product available by early-to-mid next year.",
      "article_id": "abac5bff-ab88-4b46-8139-aaeb6dab9ac3" },
    { "type": "team_pedigree", "direction": "context",
      "quote": "if there was one team to do it, the team that built Android and Stripe from the ground up would be the team",
      "article_id": "abac5bff-ab88-4b46-8139-aaeb6dab9ac3" }
  ],
  "claims_vs_metrics": "no_metrics",
  "insufficient_evidence": false,
  "label_vs_evidence_delta": "SEED at E0 — capital several stages ahead of public proof; team-pedigree check",
  "rubric_version": "v0-house",
  "model": "tbd", "classified_at": "run_ts"
}
```

The same call on Chingari's \$1.3M seed yields **E3–E4** (downloads \+ DAU \+ creator-payout mechanics) — same label, four stages apart. That spread is exactly what Loop 2 aggregates.

**Routing & cost.** House cascade pattern: flash-tier model for the bulk pass (batch pricing), escalate low-confidence / contradictory-signal rounds (~10–15%) to a Sonnet-class verifier. Tier A input is ~1–2k tokens, Tier B ~4–8k; ≈ \$0.002–0.006 per round all-in → **the full 54k backfill is low hundreds of dollars**; the 200-round POC is pocket change. _(P1 actuals came in far cheaper — see results below.)_

**Idempotency.** Keyed `(canonical_deal_id, rubric_version)` — reruns are no-ops, rubric bumps reclassify cleanly, exactly the changelog-safe shape the other Fundable lanes use.

## Loop 2 — Investor evidence profile (aggregate, no LLM)

Join classified rounds to investors: `fundable_organizations (is_investor)` ↔ `fundable_institutional_investments` **and** angel persons via `fundable_angel_investments` (angels in v1 — decided). Minimum **N = 5** classified early-stage rounds for a profile; `insufficient_evidence` rounds don't count toward N but are reported as coverage (decided).

Pure aggregation per investor: stage distribution \+ **median evidence stage**; `pre_evidence_pct` (checks at ≤ E1); `proof_pct` (checks at ≥ E3); lead vs follow split; **era-bucketed** (pre-2020 / 2020–22 / 2023\+ \+ all-time — decided; narratives lead with the current era and can cite drift); `insufficient_evidence` coverage rate; and from Layer 2, the investor's **rationale fingerprint** (team/market/product/traction mix across all their rounds, any stage).

<Warning>
  **Attribution guard (found in testing).** Incisive Ventures' investment list includes a \$20M _clinical-stage pharma convertible note_ actually led by "**Incisive Capital**" (board seat: Jonathan R. Lynch) — a same-name misjoin somewhere upstream. Guard: a round only counts toward a profile if the investor's name appears in that round's article or description text (cheap string check); failures are excluded and flagged. Without this, profiles inherit entity-resolution noise.
</Warning>

**Worked example — Incisive Ventures** (Tobias's own fund; 16 tracked institutional investments, 2 as lead; 5 sampled, stages hand-estimated at scoping time — **superseded by the P1 run below, which classified all 16**):

| Company | Label | Size | Evidence in press at raise | Est. stage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sensate (**led**) | Seed | \$3.2M | Physician-developed device shipped, "tested in clinics", subscription app live | E2 |
| Spare8 | Pre-seed | \$0.77M | "more than 30K downloads", DAU across 26 states, four weeks post-launch | E2–E3 |
| Augment AI | Seed | \$3.5M | "coming out of stealth today", product demoed to press, no customers named | E1 |
| PlayMaker | EQUITY (seed-shaped) | \$1M | "Current customers include Poppi, RCX Sports, The Bay Golf Club…" | E3 |
| Concentric Analgesics | Conv. note | \$20M | — excluded by attribution guard (Incisive _Capital_, not Ventures) | n/a |

Profile sketch this emits: median **E2**, `pre_evidence_pct` 0%, `proof_pct` 50%, leads at the bar (Sensate), 1 round guard-excluded. Note the revealed bar already _matches_ the fund's stated thesis — and now it's receipts, not marketing copy.

## Loop 3 — Narrative (per investor, LLM)

Low-volume, user-facing generation: profile \+ top-K signal quotes in, **2–4 sentence narrative \+ receipts** out. Use a stronger model — this text is read by users.

**Claim-safety rules:** every number must trace to a profile field; every qualitative claim must cite a Loop-1 signal quote (the How-I-Know receipts pattern); regenerate only on material profile change (median shift ≥ 1 stage, or ≥ 20% new rounds) so narratives stay stable.

**Worked example (hand-written at scoping time — the P1-regenerated version in the results section supersedes it):**

> **Incisive Ventures funds early-stage checks at the shipped-product bar.** Across tracked pre-seed and seed rounds, every portfolio company had a live product at announcement — half already showed usage traction or named customers — and none were pre-product bets. Receipts: led Sensate's \$3.2M seed (clinic-tested device, subscription app live); Spare8's pre-seed (30K downloads within four weeks); PlayMaker (customers including Poppi and RCX Sports at raise). Revealed evidence bar: **E2, reaching E3** — founders at deck-only stage are below this firm's bar.

That paragraph is the deliverable Tobias's thread asks founders to figure out by hand, and it generalizes to every investor with ≥ 5 tracked early-stage rounds.

**Surfaces (decided): investor profiles first** — narrative \+ expandable receipts, How-I-Know style; Discover answers cite it after ("which seed funds back pre-product founders?"); stage-alignment feature for the [Intelligence Engine](/guides/open-work/intelligence-engine) six-family scoring; [Deal Flow](/guides/open-work/roadmap/deal-flow) investor context.

## P1 POC results (2026-07-20, sandbox)

The Loop-1 classifier ran same-day over **200 stratified rounds** — 67 pre-seed, 86 seed, 39 Series A, plus the Pre-A/EQUITY/convertible-note rows in Incisive's fixed set — **200/200 classified, zero failures**. Setup: `google/gemini-3.1-flash-lite`, reasoning off, temperature 0, deal `long_description` \+ up to 2 raise-window articles (5k chars each), `rubric_version=v0-house`, `run_tag=poc-v0`. Rows live in `evidence_stage_poc` (#765, sandbox).

**Stage distribution by label** — the within-label spread is the product:

| Label | n | E0 | E1 | E2 | E3 | E4 | insufficient |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pre-seed | 67 | 13 | 18 | 9 | 13 | 13 | 1 |
| Seed | 86 | 16 | 18 | 13 | 15 | 24 | 0 |
| Series A | 39 | 1 | 3 | 3 | 9 | 23 | 0 |

19% of seeds were funded at E0 while 39% of pre-seeds already showed E3\+ proof — same labels, opposite evidence. Series A concentrates at E4, confirming the scope rule (A is where evidence goes KPI-legible) while exposing a 4-round conviction tail at ≤E1. Nothing scored E5 — the v0 E5 bar may be set too high (revisit at the Tobias-thread rubric merge).

**What drove the checks** (the investor\_rationale extraction): **115/200 rounds** carried at least one explicitly investor-attributed rationale quote — 203 quotes typed **market 67 · product 59 · team 44 · traction 23 · other 10**. Early-stage investors publicly justify checks with market and product far more than traction — which makes demonstrated-traction funding (the E3/E4 cohort) the sharpest narrative differentiator an investor can own.

**Quality stats.** `insufficient_evidence`: 1/200. `claims_vs_metrics`: **claims\_only 119 / metrics 76 / none 5** — ~60% of early-stage funding press contains zero hard numbers, exactly the puffery rule 1 guards against. Confidence came back "high" on 198/200 — flash-tier over-confidence; calibration is now an explicit P2 target. (Sample caveat: ids drawn from article-bearing id-order pages, so 0/200 windowless rounds is not a corpus-wide coverage estimate.)

**Incisive Ventures — real Loop-2 preview.** All 16 tracked rounds classified: **median E4, 13/16 at ≥E3, 2 at ≤E1, zero E0.** The scoping-time hand estimate ("E2, reaching E3") undersold the fund — the receipts: TurnStay (ZAR 250M in transactions processed), Moddule (6x revenue growth), Cuéntame (64K users across 30 named enterprises), Spare8 (30K downloads in four weeks), PlayMaker (five named clients). Regenerated narrative:

> **Incisive Ventures writes pre-seed and seed checks on demonstrated traction.** Across 16 tracked early-stage rounds, 13 showed named customers, revenue growth, or usage at scale in press at announcement — and none were team-and-idea-only bets. Receipts: Spare8's pre-seed (30K downloads within four weeks of launch), Moddule's pre-seed (6x revenue growth), TurnStay's seed (ZAR 250M processed), PlayMaker (five named clients including Poppi and RCX Sports). Revealed evidence bar: **E3–E4** — founders without live traction are below this firm's demonstrated bar.

(The attribution guard still applies: the Concentric note — "Incisive Capital", not Ventures — classified cleanly at E2 but must not count toward this profile.)

**Delta highlights across the sample:**

- _Capital ahead of proof — seeds at E0, by size:_ /dev/agents \$56M, Carmoola \$35.2M, Fractile \$15.4M, Next Insurance \$13M, 1001 AI \$9M. Every rationale: team pedigree \+ market size.
- _Outperformer flags — pre-seeds at E4:_ Vint (collections sell out within 48 hours), Cuéntame, Moddule, Ark Climate (43 German municipalities live), Crink.
- _Conviction As — Series A at ≤E1:_ Protego Biopharma \$51M, Promise \$20M, Odyssey \$18M — pedigree-driven As with no public traction evidence.

**Cost actuals.** Avg 1,776 tokens in / 249 out per round → the 200-round run cost ≈ **\$0.06** at flash-lite list pricing; a full 54k backfill extrapolates to **~\$15–25**, an order of magnitude under the scoping estimate (the capped two-article window keeps inputs small).

**Flags the POC raised for P0/P2:** confidence calibration (198/200 high); era spread (the sample includes 2007-vintage rounds — era-bucketed profiles now decided); EQUITY rounds classified cleanly when included (PlayMaker E3, Luna E4, Vega E3) — EQUITY cut now decided; quote fidelity (verbatim-ness of extracted signal quotes) joins the hand-label checklist.

## Eval & guardrails

- **Hand-label 100–150 rounds** (stratified pre-seed / seed / Series A) before trusting anything at scale: exact \+ adjacent-stage agreement, per-stage confusion matrix. Gate for backfill: ≥ 85% adjacent agreement. Seed the label set with the bake-off disagreement worklist. Run prompts through the LLM Test Harness (API group 42) like every other pipeline prompt.
- Standing guards: point-in-time window; syndicate dedupe; claims-vs-metrics; `insufficient_evidence` ≠ E0; attribution guard; floor semantics (never present the stage as the company's _actual_ stage).
