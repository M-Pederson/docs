## The one correction: OpenUI is already live

The current-state audit states:

> **No OpenUI.** `@openuidev/*` is absent from `package.json`, `src/`, `node_modules`, and `pnpm-lock.yaml`.

That is not what the repo shows.

<Warning>
**`@openuidev/*` is installed and actively rendering production surfaces.** The audit appears to have been scoped to `src/features/copilot/` (which is Crayon) and then generalized to the whole repo.
</Warning>

**Installed** (`package.json`):

```json
"@openuidev/react-lang": "^0.2.4",
"@openuidev/react-ui": "^0.11.6",
"@openuidev/react-headless": "^0.8.1"
```

**Live in `src/`** — `<Renderer>` from `@openuidev/react-lang` (9 import sites) drives:

## Why it matters for Scope B

Scope B is framed as a *future, pre-1.0 bet* whose headline risk is *"betting the most central surface on a pre-1.0 renderer stack."* But the team already ships **four** user-facing surfaces on exactly that stack — including a feature dogfooded end-to-end today. So:

- Scope B isn't **greenfield OpenUI adoption** — it's **converging the one remaining Crayon surface (the copilot) onto the renderer the rest of the app already runs.**
- The "unproven pre-1.0" risk is materially lower than stated: the pre-1.0 stack is already in production here.
- The "clean end-state" (AI SDK orchestration + OpenUI render) is **already ~80% reached on the render side** — only the copilot is still on Crayon.

This sharpens the sequencing story rather than overturning it: Scope A still comes first (streaming must be fixed at the orchestration layer regardless of renderer), and `cards.ts` is still the seam.
